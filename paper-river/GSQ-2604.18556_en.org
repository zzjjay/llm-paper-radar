* River of Questions

A 70B-parameter model in full precision occupies 140GB of GPU memory — too large for a single card, and entirely out of reach for consumer-grade hardware. Squeezing each weight down to 2-3 bits would let the model fit on a single 24GB consumer GPU.

But low-precision compression has a peculiar split. The field is divided into two camps. *Scalar quantization* rounds each weight independently to an entry in a tiny number table — simple, supported by existing inference kernels, and the foundation of GGUF / llama.cpp / a whole shipping ecosystem. *Vector / trellis quantization* packs groups of weights into a codebook — higher accuracy, but requires new lookup-table kernels and rewriting the deployment stack.

The tension: at 2-3 bpp, the scalar camp's accuracy collapses while the vector camp leads by a wide margin. Yet nobody wants to rewrite their inference stack just for that accuracy bump.
