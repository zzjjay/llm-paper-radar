#### Summary
While quantized LLMs achieve strong performance on unperturbed inputs, their reliability under perturbations—spanning uncertainty, calibration, and robustness—remains understudied. This work presents a comprehensive reliability evaluation of LLMs quantized to 2, 3, 4, and 8 bits using six quantization methods, assessing uncertainty trustworthiness, calibration of uncertainty estimates across scales and precisions, and robustness to character- and word-level input perturbations. Key findings show that predictive performance scales monotonically with total model bits, but reliability scaling is nonlinear, with a peak at 4-bit quantization; additionally, quantization is found to enhance robustness to natural input perturbations.

- 🎯 Comprehensive reliability evaluation across 2/3/4/8-bit quantization using six methods, measuring uncertainty, calibration, and robustness
- 📊 Reliability scaling is nonlinear with total model bits; 4-bit quantized models achieve the best reliability-efficiency trade-off
- 💡 Quantization empirically enhances LLM robustness to natural character-level and word-level input perturbations
- ⚠️ No end-to-end inference speedup reported; standard downstream benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K) not used as primary comparison dimensions
