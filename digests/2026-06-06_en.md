#### Summary
Under test-time scaling with long-horizon autoregressive decoding, KV-cache quantization errors accumulate across timesteps, primarily driven by incorrect token scales — a regime missed by prefill-style evaluation. KVarN is a calibration-free KV-cache quantizer that applies a Hadamard rotation followed by dual-axis variance normalization on the K and V matrices, fixing outlying token-scale errors and reducing accumulated error. At 2-bit precision it sets a new SOTA on generative benchmarks including MATH500, AIME24 and HumanEval, with a vLLM implementation released.

- 💡 Identifies token-scale errors as the main driver of cross-timestep error accumulation in decoding
- 🎯 Hadamard rotation + dual-axis variance normalization, calibration-free 2-bit KV quantization
- 📊 New SOTA on MATH500, AIME24, HumanEval at 2-bit KV-cache
- 🔧 vLLM implementation released as open source
