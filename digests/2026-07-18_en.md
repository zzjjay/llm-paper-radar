#### Summary
Large MoE models are inaccessible on most developer hardware because their expert pools encode far more capabilities than a coding-focused user needs. This work applies one-shot expert pruning to Qwen3-6-35B-A3B and Gemma-4-26B-A4B under five selection strategies, evaluating on real coding benchmarks rather than perplexity. Half the experts can be removed from either model with no statistically detectable loss on the primary code benchmark, with damage concentrated outside coding; however, the winning strategy differs between the two model families, precluding cross-family recipe transfer. Perplexity is shown to be an unreliable proxy, pruning beats quantization only below 3 bits per weight, and an agentic repair evaluation reveals that single-shot benchmarks broadly overstate compression penalties—one repair turn erases the 2-bit quantization penalty entirely.
