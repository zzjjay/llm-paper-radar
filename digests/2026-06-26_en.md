#### Summary
ROCKET compresses LLMs via sparse-dictionary factorization guided by a multi-choice knapsack problem (MCKP) allocation that uses weight-space Frobenius error as its cost. This paper investigates whether replacing that cost with an output-space reconstruction objective (ROCKET-ActCost) improves compressed model quality. On Qwen3-8B at 50% compression, ROCKET-ActCost gains +0.8 pp average accuracy across 8 zero-shot benchmarks (53.1% vs 52.3%) but increases WikiText perplexity by 16% (61.46 vs 52.98), revealing an accuracy-PPL tradeoff. On Llama-3.2-1B at 20% compression, the two methods yield near-identical results, and the >0.99 correlation between weight-space and output-space errors explains the overall modest effect size.

- 📊 Llama-3.2-1B at 20% compression: near-identical results (53.3% vs 53.5% accuracy, 14.45 vs 14.66 PPL)
- 💡 Weight-space and output-space errors correlate at >0.99, limiting allocation divergence and cost-function impact at lower compression ratios
