#### Summary
Existing KV cache compression methods rely on attention weights to assess token importance, missing information-theoretic signals such as predictive uncertainty. This paper introduces Forward Influence, a metric quantifying how compressed tokens affect future contexts, and finds that high-uncertainty tokens exert substantially stronger influence on distant future contexts than attention-selected tokens. InfoKV, an entropy-aware compression framework, combines token-level predictive uncertainty with layer-wise representation evolution and integrates the resulting entropy scores with attention scores during reasoning. Experiments on long-context reasoning benchmarks with Llama-3.1, Llama-3.2, and DeepSeek-R1 show InfoKV consistently outperforms existing attention-based KV compression methods in both long prefilling and decoding scenarios.

- 🎯 Forward Influence metric quantifies how compressed tokens impact near vs. distant future contexts
- 💡 High-predictive-uncertainty tokens strongly influence distant contexts; attention scores favor nearby contexts
- 🔧 InfoKV integrates entropy scores with attention scores, covering both long prefilling and decoding
- 📊 Outperforms attention-based KV compression on Llama-3.1/3.2 and DeepSeek-R1; no end-to-end speedup reported

#### Summary
KV cache memory fragmentation and dynamic growth severely limit batch sizes in LLM serving, capping throughput. PagedAttention adapts classical OS virtual memory and paging to manage KV cache in non-contiguous physical blocks, achieving near-zero memory waste and enabling flexible KV cache sharing within and across requests. Built on PagedAttention, vLLM delivers 2–4× higher throughput than FasterTransformer and Orca at the same latency level, with gains amplified for longer sequences, larger models, and complex decoding algorithms.

- 🎯 PagedAttention maps KV cache to non-contiguous physical memory blocks, eliminating fragmentation and redundant duplication
