#### Summary
GR3D is a spatial vision-language model unifying three grounding capabilities--explicit 2D grounding, implicit 2D grounding, and monocular 3D grounding--in a single framework. The implicit grounding mechanism detects entity mentions during generation and inserts region tokens into the text stream, letting the model cite visual evidence on the fly within spatial chain-of-thought responses. #### Summary
CONF-KV tackles KV-cache bloat in long-horizon LLM inference by turning the per-step next-token distribution into a scalar confidence score that sets the cache budget: keep more context when uncertain, prune aggressively when confident. Within each budget, tokens are ranked by combined accumulated attention mass and recency, with a protected recent window for local coherence, paired with blockwise online-softmax attention, mixed FP16/INT8 storage, and a pyramidal per-layer budget. Across four model families up to 4K generated tokens, CONF-KV matches a 512-token sliding-window footprint while staying within 1.5-2.1 PPL of full KV. On 32K Needle-in-a-Haystack it hits 91.4% retrieval (vs 53.8% sliding, 80.6% H2O), and on 75 VisualWebArena tasks retains 95.3% of full-KV success at 2.8x lower peak memory.

- 💡 Next-token distribution confidence dynamically sets per-step KV budget
- 🔧 Mixed FP16/INT8 storage + pyramidal per-layer budget + online-softmax
- 📊 32K NIAH: 91.4% vs sliding 53.8%, H2O 80.6%
- 📊 VisualWebArena: 95.3% of full-KV success at 2.8x lower peak memory
