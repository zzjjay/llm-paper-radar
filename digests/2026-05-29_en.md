#### Summary
Revisits the role of temperature τ in LLM distillation and shows it fundamentally changes the FKL vs RKL comparison. Analysis reveals an asymmetric effect: temperature enriches FKL with non-dominant token signals, while it mainly rescales RKL gradients. Consequently, although RKL beats FKL at τ=1, FKL consistently surpasses RKL at higher temperatures on instruction-following benchmarks. Temperature also benefits a broader family of distillation objectives, letting simple KL-based methods rival recent SOTA LLM distillation approaches.
