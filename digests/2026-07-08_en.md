#### Summary
Existing mobile video diffusion models are limited to 0.4-1.8B parameters, constraining generation quality. MobileWan starts from the server-scale Wan2.2-5B and deploys it on memory-constrained mobile hardware via a recurrence distillation framework that converts generation into a chunk-wise autoregressive process with constant-memory attention, using causal linear attention so the model runs as an RNN at inference. It further introduces learnable attention head pruning with binary per-head gates and a noise-biased sparsity objective, combined with sampling-step distillation and memory-optimized VAE decoding. The resulting system is the first 5B-scale video diffusion model deployed on a commercial mobile device, generating 5-second 480×832 videos at 16 FPS with 20-second end-to-end latency and achieving a VBench score of 83.79.

#### Summary
