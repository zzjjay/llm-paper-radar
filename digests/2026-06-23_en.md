#### Summary
Existing KV-cache quantizers treat cached keys as flat vectors, overlooking the block-structured contribution of RoPE frequency blocks to attention logits. Block-GTQ, built on TurboQuant-MSE (TQ-MSE), computes a label-free energy score per RoPE block for each layer and KV head, then greedily allocates integer bit widths by marginal gain, prioritizing high-energy blocks under a fixed K/V bit budget. On a ten-model diagnostic panel, Block-GTQ cuts per-layer MAE by 32–80% at 2 and 3 b/dim K-only quantization and wins all 367/367 layer comparisons against uniform TQ-MSE. At K2V2 on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, NIAH average rises from 70.6 to 97.4 and LongBench-EN average from 36.87 to 53.31; a packed K3V3 serving path on a single H800 GPU achieves 3.24× KV-cache compression, 1.34× speedup over fp16 FlashAttention2 at 128K context, and reduces peak memory from 56.31 GB to 19.85 GB.
