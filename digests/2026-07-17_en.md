#### Summary
Rollout generation is a major bottleneck in MoE LLM RL training; NVFP4 (W4A4) offers higher throughput than FP8 but collapses after ~150 training steps when applied naively, with root cause traced to activation quantization error amplified by the coarse E2M1 grid rather than weight error. QUADS addresses this with a two-sided approach: Asymmetric QAT on the trainer side fake-quantizes weights while keeping activations in BF16, and Residual Activation Compensation on the rollout side corrects high-error activation channels while preserving native W4A4 GEMMs. Across multiple RL benchmarks, QUADS achieves BF16-level accuracy, improves average pass@1 by 21.49 points over naive NVFP4 RL, and delivers ~16% higher rollout throughput than FP8.
