#### Summary
Current FP4 training hardware (NVIDIA Blackwell, AMD MI350) centers on the E2M1 format, which suffers from Shrinkage Bias—a systematic negative rounding error caused by the geometric asymmetry of its representable bins. This bias accumulates multiplicatively across layers and is further amplified by the Random Hadamard Transform (RHT), providing a unified explanation for the training instability seen in E2M1-based recipes. The authors propose UFP4, a uniform 4-bit training recipe that applies RHT to all three training GEMMs and restricts stochastic rounding to dY only, using E1M2/INT4 uniform grids instead. UFP4 consistently achieves lower BF16-relative loss degradation than strong E2M1 baselines on Dense 1.5B, MoE 7.9B, and MoE 124B long-run pretraining, backed by scaling-law analysis and ablation studies.
