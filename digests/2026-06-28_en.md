#### Summary
Deploying LLMs with long-context inputs incurs substantial memory overhead from storing the KV cache. Existing KV-cache eviction strategies exploit attention sparsity to drop tokens, but this reduces unique-token coverage and, as shown theoretically, limits mutual information between inputs and outputs, degrading accuracy. K-VEC addresses this with a coverage-aware eviction strategy featuring cross-head and cross-layer coverage modules that prioritize token diversity during eviction. Under the same eviction rate and memory constraints, K-VEC achieves up to 10.35-point improvement over existing methods across 16 LongBench subsets.
