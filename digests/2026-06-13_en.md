#### Summary
Addressing that layer-wise sparsity allocation in LLMs relies on local layer importance while ignoring the network's downstream compensatory capacity, the authors directly characterize pruning-scale perturbation absorption via controlled experiments. They find early layers amplify perturbations while middle and late layers absorb them, with relative L2 drift decreasing monotonically across depth; absorption is a large-perturbation phenomenon, transitioning smoothly from universal amplification under small perturbations as magnitude grows to pruning scale, enriching the linearized accumulation theory of prior work. They define a per-layer absorption coefficient and propose absorption-aware correction, an orthogonal augmentation to OWL and AlphaPruning that reduces perplexity by 7.13% and boosts zero-shot accuracy by 1.02% across multiple model families at 70% sparsity.
