#### 🌊 Paper River
[Back-read critique chain](../paper-river/SubFit-2606.02559_en.org)

#### Summary
Existing replacement-based LLM compression methods are constrained to full-layer granularity with contiguous selection, but redundancy is not uniform across Attention vs FeedForward nor confined to contiguous depth ranges. SubFit compresses at the submodule level: Attention and FeedForward submodules are selected non-contiguously, each fitted with its own lightweight residual bypass. It is post-training and requires only calibration data. Across 10 LLMs (5 base, 5 instruction-tuned), 5 sparsity levels (12.5%-37.5%), and 4 replacement-based baselines, SubFit achieves the best aggregate perplexity-accuracy trade-off; at 25% sparsity it retains 84.6% of dense downstream accuracy with 2.42x PPL degradation, vs 81.6% and 4.34x for the strongest baseline, plus inference speedup and KV-cache savings.

- 💡 Breaks full-layer and contiguous-selection constraints via submodule-level non-contiguous replacement
- 🎯 Separate fitted residual bypass per Attention/FFN submodule
- 📊 At 25% sparsity: 84.6% accuracy retained, 2.42x PPL vs baseline 81.6%/4.34x
- 🔧 Post-training, calibration-only, with measurable speedup and KV-cache savings

#### 🌊 Paper River
[Back-read critique chain](../paper-river/DFlare-2606.02091_en.org)

#### Summary
DFlare addresses the conditioning bottleneck in DFlash for block diffusion speculative decoding, where all draft layers share a single fused representation from only a few target layers, limiting draft capacity scaling. It introduces a lightweight layer-wise fusion: each draft layer attends to its own learnable combination over a broad set of target layers, injecting richer target knowledge with negligible overhead and giving every draft layer a distinct input. Training data is scaled from 800K to 2.4M samples to exploit the enlarged capacity. Across six math, code, and conversation benchmarks, DFlare achieves 5.52x, 5.46x, and 3.91x wall-clock speedups on Qwen3-4B, Qwen3-8B, and GPT-OSS-20B, beating DFlash by ~11%, 8%, and 5%.

- 💡 Layer-wise fusion replaces DFlash's single fused bottleneck representation
- 🎯 Each draft layer learns its own combination of broad target layers with negligible overhead
- 📊 5.52x/5.46x speedup on Qwen3-4B/8B, +11%/+8% over DFlash
- 🔧 Training data scaled from 800K to 2.4M samples; code open-sourced

#### 📎 Related methods / baselines
- DFlash — extended with layer-wise fusion, 5-11% faster
