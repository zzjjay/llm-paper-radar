#### Summary
Existing low-bit quantization methods for CPU inference either provide coarse operating points or mixed-precision schemes that are hard to execute efficiently. PolyQ is a CPU-oriented compiler/quantization co-design that assigns per-channel bit-widths from {2,3,4,8,16} under a user-specified average-bit budget, then uses a compile-time compiler to cluster channels into bit-homogeneous blocks, generate SIMD- and LUT-compatible kernels, and merge permutations across operators to eliminate runtime layout overhead. On WikiText-2 across Falcon-H1-3B, Llama2-13B, and Qwen3-32B, PolyQ improves perplexity by 2.4–32.1% over prior methods at a 3b target, with activation reorder traffic reduced by up to 70.8% and energy/token overhead below 2% relative to an optimized LUT-based back-end across workstation, laptop, and mobile CPUs.
