#### Summary
To address KV cache dominating bandwidth and memory in long-context autoregressive inference, OCTOPUS extends the rotation-preconditioned quantization paradigm (TurboQuant, PolarQuant) by jointly quantizing triplets of rotated coordinates: each triplet's direction is mapped to a square via octahedral parameterization, and the two resulting coordinates plus the triplet norm are Lloyd-Max quantized against implementation-matched marginals. #### Summary
EntmaxKV is a sparse decoding framework tailored to α-entmax attention that targets the KV-cache memory-traffic bottleneck of long-context decoding. Unlike softmax-based sparse decoders that approximate dense tails, it exploits entmax's exact zeros, turning sparse decoding into a support-recovery problem: if selected candidates cover the entmax support, decoding is exact. The method combines query-aware page scoring, support-aware candidate selection, sparse entmax attention, and a Gaussian-aware selector that estimates the entmax threshold from lightweight page statistics to adapt the budget. Theoretically, output error is bounded by the dropped probability mass δ. Empirically, EntmaxKV drops less mass and achieves lower error than softmax sparse decoding at matched KV budgets, with up to 3.36× (softmax) and 5.43× (entmax) speedup over full attention at 1M context length.
