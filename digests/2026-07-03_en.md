#### Summary
Existing low-bit quantization treats bit-width as a single global hyperparameter shared across all weights. VBQ is a training-time method where each contiguous group of 64 weights learns its own precision from {1,2,4,8} bits via Gumbel-Softmax relaxation, jointly trained through alternating optimization that provides a task-aligned signal to precision logits. The learned allocation is strongly heterogeneous: 69% of groups collapse to 1 bit while early MLP blocks retain ~2.5 bits, and deeper layers progressively self-heal quantization error from earlier layers. VBQ achieves 3.9–8.4x quality-per-byte over FP16, and on Apple Silicon delivers up to 4.7x autoregressive decode speedup at 9B scale.
