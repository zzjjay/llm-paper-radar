#### Summary
Low-bit quantization conventionally fixes a single global bit-width for all weights. VBQ introduces a training-time approach in which each contiguous group of 64 weights learns its own resolution from {1,2,4,8} bits via Gumbel-Softmax relaxation, jointly trained with an alternating optimization that gives precision logits a task-aligned signal. The discovered allocation is strongly heterogeneous—69% of groups collapse to 1 bit, the LM head averages 1.09 bits, and the first MLP block retains ~2.5 bits—and stable enough to freeze into a reusable recipe. A 1.46B model matches a 593M FP16 control at ~3.7x less storage (3.9–8.4x better quality-per-byte than FP16), and custom fused dequantize-and-multiply kernels deliver 1.9x decode speedup at 1B and 4.7x at 9B on Apple silicon.

- 🎯 Each group of 64 weights learns its bit-width from {1,2,4,8} via Gumbel-Softmax and alternating optimization at train time
- 📊 1.46B model matches 593M FP16 at ~3.7x less storage; quality-per-byte is 3.9–8.4x better than FP16
- 🔧 Custom fused kernels yield 1.9x (1B) and 4.7x (9B) autoregressive decode speedup on Apple silicon
- ⚠️ Requires full from-scratch QAT; evaluated only on PPL (TinyStories/FineWeb-Edu); scaling search beyond 1.5B remains open
