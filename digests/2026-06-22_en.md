#### Summary
HyperQuant is a unified post-training quantization (PTQ) pipeline for weights and KV cache of large language and diffusion transformers. It combines four techniques: per-tile Randomized Hadamard Transform (RHT) to Gaussianize weight/activation distributions, quantization to optimal lattices (E8, D4, A2, Z), near-entropy-optimal Rice coding of lattice indices, and bias-correction for KV cache inner products. HyperQuant outperforms HIGGS at every weight-quantization operating point from 3 to 5 bps, beats TurboQuant and OCTOPUS on KV quantization down to 1.7 bps, and achieves ~3.9x weight compression and ~3.79x KV cache compression at near-lossless quality on an H100 at 4 bps, with successful application to the 19B-parameter LTX-2 DiT video model.

- 📊 Result: ~3.9x weight compression and ~3.79x KV cache compression at near-lossless quality on H100 at 4 bps
- 📊 Result: outperforms HIGGS at all 3–5 bps operating points; beats TurboQuant and OCTOPUS at KV quantization down to 1.7 bps
- 🔧 Engineering: integrated with fp8-e4m3, int8, nvfp4, mxfp4 Tensor-Core MMA paths; int8 outperforms fp8 on post-RHT lattice output

#### Summary
Autoregressive LLM decoding is bottlenecked by GPU memory bandwidth, and existing quantization methods fail to distinguish the compute-bound prefill stage from the memory-bound decoding stage. GRINQH is a weight-only post-training quantization framework that uses activation magnitudes as a proxy for computational importance to dynamically assign weight channels to different precision levels, unifying quantization and sparsification. On Llama3 and Qwen3, GRINQH outperforms fixed- and mixed-precision baselines at 3-bit and 4-bit settings and enables effective 2-bit generation, with theoretical speedups verified experimentally via a custom GPU kernel using a hierarchical nested memory layout.

- 🎯 Weight-only PTQ dynamically assigns weight channels to multiple precision levels using activation magnitudes, enabling flexible average bit widths.
- 💡 Exploits prefill/decode asymmetry by unifying quantization and sparsification, establishing a new Pareto frontier for generation quality vs. speed.
- 📊 Outperforms state-of-the-art fixed- and mixed-precision baselines at 3-bit and 4-bit on Llama3 and Qwen3; enables effective 2-bit generation.
- 🔧 Custom GPU kernel with hierarchical nested multi-precision memory layout experimentally validates theoretical decoding speedups.

#### Summary
Deploying LLMs in resource-constrained environments is hindered by high computational cost; Knowledge Distillation (KD) addresses this by transferring knowledge from a large teacher to a smaller student. Using the large-scale Tulu 3 dataset, this work systematically studies KD effectiveness in the post-training stage: KD outperforms SFT in low-data regimes, but the gap narrows as training data grows, unless the teacher is a stronger instruction-tuned model. For domain-specific, low-resource scenarios, a two-stage KD strategy is proposed — first generating synthetic teacher-labeled data, then refining on human annotations — which consistently improves student performance.
