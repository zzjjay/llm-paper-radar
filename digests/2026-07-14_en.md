#### Summary
Mixed-precision quantization relies on the assumption that joint quantization loss across layers can be reconstructed from per-layer or pairwise sensitivities measured in isolation—a premise shared by methods such as HAWQ and CoopQ. This work treats the loss change f(S) from quantizing a layer set S as a set function on the Boolean cube and analyzes it via two classical basis transforms at 4-bit weight-and-activation precision, finding that per-layer effects alone explain 85–93% of f's variance and that a monotone transform of a per-layer sum misorders at most 2% of configuration pairs. The proposed coverage model f(S)=c(1−∏(1−aᵢ)) reproduces the measured variance profile of f with only L+1 fitted parameters, and Parseval's identity provides a certificate bounding the best achievable accuracy of any additive model. At matched memory budgets, the resulting allocators achieve the lowest KL divergence among all compared methods on models from 30B to 355B parameters, and remain functional on code and reasoning tasks below four bits where gradient-sensitivity allocators fail to produce terminating generations.

#### Summary
The KV cache is the dominant memory bottleneck in transformer inference, yet existing low-rank and quantization methods fail to exploit its natural third-order tensor structure across heads, tokens, and features. JoLT applies a partial Tucker decomposition to compress only the token and feature axes, then recovers truncated energy via a Johnson-Lindenstrauss (JL) rotated low-bit residual. A single Lagrangian dual jointly allocates Tucker ranks and residual bit-widths per layer group (keys and values separately) under a unified byte budget. On both Mistral-7B-v0.3 and LLaMA-2-13B, JoLT achieves near-lossless 2-3x compression with relative Frobenius errors of 0.009 (K) and 0.006 (V)—roughly an order of magnitude below cross-layer SVD and 4-bit quantization—while perplexity, GSM8K accuracy, and RULER retrieval remain within statistical noise of the uncompressed baseline; a randomized-SVD variant, FlashJoLT, delivers 5-13x compression-time speedup at matched quality.

- 🎯 Models KV cache as a third-order tensor; applies partial Tucker decomposition on token and feature axes plus a JL-rotated low-bit residual to recover truncated energy
- 💡 Single Lagrangian dual jointly allocates Tucker ranks and residual bit-widths per layer group under one byte budget, separately for keys and values
- 📊 At 2x compression, relative Frobenius errors are 0.009 (K) and 0.006 (V)—~10× lower than cross-layer SVD and 4-bit quantization; GSM8K and RULER stay within statistical noise of baseline
- 🔧 FlashJoLT (randomized-SVD variant) achieves 5-13x compression-time speedup at matched quality; end-to-end inference throughput not reported

- cross-layer SVD — low-rank baseline; Frobenius error ~10× higher than JoLT
