#### Summary
JacQuant addresses the unreliable gradients of the Straight-Through Estimator (STE) near quantizer bin boundaries in ≤2-bit LLM QAT. It learns a lightweight diagonal or block-diagonal Jacobian surrogate of the model's local sensitivity to parameter changes and uses it inside standard variance-reduced optimizers in place of STE gradients. The authors prove convergence for non-convex objectives, a linear rate under a PL condition, and link the learned sensitivity to end-to-end output fidelity via a calibration argument. On ≤2-bit LLM benchmarks JacQuant consistently outperforms STE-based QAT, with negligible runtime overhead under practical group sizes, and is drop-in with no changes to the forward weight/activation quantizers.

- 🎯 Diagonal/block-diagonal surrogate, drop-in over existing quantizers
