#### Summary

#### Summary
Efficient Distillation (EDistill) compresses LLMs via structured pruning and lightweight module tuning, but suffers severe multi-step reasoning degradation termed reasoning collapse. The authors trace this to eRank collapse: randomly initialized width-reducing projection matrices yield unevenly distributed singular values in hidden representations, making tokens indistinguishable. RED introduces activation-aware initialization that sets projection matrices as channel-selection matrices, theoretically mitigating eRank collapse. Experiments on Llama and Qwen series recover reasoning ability while preserving SOTA general performance and high training efficiency.
