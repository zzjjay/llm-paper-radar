#### Summary
Long-horizon decoding for test-time scaling is bottlenecked by a growing KV-cache, but existing KV-cache quantizers are evaluated in prefill-like settings and ignore how errors compound under autoregressive decoding. The authors show that this accumulation is driven mainly by incorrect token scales. KVarN is a calibration-free quantizer that applies a Hadamard rotation followed by dual-scaling variance normalization across both axes of K and V, fixing outlier token-scale errors and curbing error accumulation. At 2-bit precision it sets a new state-of-the-art on MATH500, AIME24 and HumanEval, with a vLLM implementation released.

- 💡 Identifies token-scale-driven KV quantization error accumulation across decoding steps
- 🎯 Hadamard rotation + dual-axis variance normalization on K/V, calibration-free
- 📊 New SOTA at 2-bit KV cache on MATH500, AIME24, HumanEval
- 🔧 vLLM implementation open-sourced
