#### Summary
Autoregressive video diffusion with sliding-window attention suffers from accumulated errors and identity drift in long-horizon generation, since a degraded window forces all subsequent generations to condition on a corrupted trajectory. LongLive-RAG reframes long video generation as retrieval-augmented generation: previously generated latents form a dynamic, searchable history, and each new block uses a query embedding to retrieve relevant historical latents as non-local context.
