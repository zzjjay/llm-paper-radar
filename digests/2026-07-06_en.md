#### Summary
KV cache growth scales linearly with context length and constitutes a major bottleneck in autoregressive decoding, while existing static heuristic eviction methods poorly track future token utility. KVpop learns a fixed-budget KV eviction policy by directly supervising keep-or-drop decisions using a novel future-attention target computed without materializing dense attention maps. A delayed memory-based scorer is also introduced, deferring scoring by a fixed number of steps to exploit near-future context — a unique feature among learned eviction methods. #### Summary
DiT activations shift across timesteps, prompts, and guidance branches, requiring prior PTQ methods to re-fit calibration data for each new checkpoint or modality. OrbitQuant applies a randomized permuted block-Hadamard (RPBH) rotation to transform activations into a normalized basis where each coordinate's marginal distribution converges to a fixed, known shape, allowing a single Lloyd-Max codebook to cover all timesteps, prompts, and layers of a given input dimension. The rotation is absorbed offline into weight rows, leaving only a forward rotation on activations at runtime. No calibration data is required, and the same recipe transfers from image to video DiTs without per-modality tuning, achieving PTQ state of the art at several low-bit settings across FLUX.1, Z-Image-Turbo, Wan 2.1, and CogVideoX, and pushing image DiT PTQ to W2A4 with usable generation quality.

- 🎯 RPBH rotation normalizes activation distributions so one Lloyd-Max codebook covers all timesteps, prompts, and layers—no calibration data needed
- 💡 Rotation absorbed offline into weights; only a forward rotation on activations remains at runtime, minimizing deployment overhead
- 📊 Achieves PTQ SOTA at multiple low-bit settings on FLUX.1, Z-Image-Turbo, Wan 2.1, and CogVideoX
- 📊 Pushes image diffusion transformer PTQ to W2A4 with usable generation quality
