#### Summary
KV cache growth scales linearly with context length and bottlenecks autoregressive decoding; existing eviction methods rely on static heuristics or proxy scores that poorly track future token utility. KVpop learns a fixed-budget KV eviction policy by directly supervising keep-or-drop decisions against a novel future-attention target, computed efficiently without materializing dense attention maps. #### Summary
Diffusion Transformer (DiT) inference is costly due to multi-step sampling and growing parameter counts, while existing PTQ methods require re-fitting calibration data per checkpoint because activations shift across timesteps, prompts, and guidance branches. OrbitQuant introduces a data-agnostic weight-activation quantizer that applies a randomized permuted block-Hadamard (RPBH) rotation to map activations into a normalized basis where each coordinate concentrates around a fixed, known marginal distribution, enabling a single Lloyd-Max codebook to serve all timesteps, prompts, and layers of a given input dimension. The rotation is absorbed into weights offline so that only a forward rotation on activations remains at runtime, and the same recipe transfers from image to video DiTs without per-modality tuning. Evaluated on FLUX.1, Z-Image-Turbo, Wan 2.1, and CogVideoX, OrbitQuant achieves state-of-the-art PTQ at several low-bit settings and extends image DiT quantization to W2A4 with usable generation quality.

- 🎯 RPBH rotation normalizes activations to a fixed marginal distribution, allowing one Lloyd-Max codebook to cover all timesteps, prompts, and layers with no calibration data
- 💡 Rotation absorbed offline into weights; runtime overhead is only a single forward rotation on activations
- 📊 Achieves PTQ SOTA at multiple low-bit settings on FLUX.1, Z-Image-Turbo, Wan 2.1, and CogVideoX; pushes image DiT to W2A4 with usable quality
- 🔧 Single recipe transfers from image to video DiTs with zero per-modality tuning
