#### Summary
KV cache growth scales linearly with context length, creating a major bottleneck in autoregressive decoding. KVpop learns a fixed-budget KV eviction policy by directly supervising keep-or-drop decisions against a novel future-attention target computed without materializing dense attention maps. A delayed memory-based scorer further defers scoring for a fixed number of steps to leverage near-future context during inference. On AIME and HMMT mathematical reasoning benchmarks, KVpop retains 98% of full-attention performance on Qwen3-4B at 75% KV cache compression and 97% at 88% compression, with Qwen3-8B reaching near-full teacher performance.

#### Summary
Diffusion transformer (DiT) activations shift across timesteps, prompts, and guidance branches, forcing prior PTQ methods to re-fit calibration data for each new checkpoint or modality. OrbitQuant applies a randomized permuted block-Hadamard (RPBH) rotation to project activations into a normalized basis where each coordinate follows a fixed, known marginal distribution, allowing a single Lloyd-Max codebook to serve all timesteps, prompts, and layers of a given input dimension without any calibration data. The rotation is absorbed offline into weight rows, leaving only a forward rotation on activations at runtime. Evaluated on FLUX.1, Z-Image-Turbo, Wan 2.1, and CogVideoX, OrbitQuant achieves state-of-the-art PTQ at several low-bit settings and extends image DiT quantization to W2A4 with usable generation quality.

- 🎯 RPBH rotation normalizes activations to a fixed marginal; one Lloyd-Max codebook covers all timesteps, prompts, and layers — no calibration data needed
- 💡 Rotation absorbed offline into weights, canceling inside each linear layer; only a forward activation rotation remains at runtime
- 📊 Sets PTQ SOTA across FLUX.1, Z-Image-Turbo, Wan 2.1, and CogVideoX at multiple low-bit settings; pushes image DiT PTQ to W2A4
- ⚠️ No end-to-end inference speedup reported; activation rotation introduces additional runtime overhead
