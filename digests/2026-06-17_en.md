#### Summary
Spatial VLMs still struggle with complex multi-step spatial reasoning over depth, distance, and scene relations, and different query types demand fundamentally different strategies. SR-REAL introduces a dual-path framework: Language-Only Reasoning (LOR) performs step-by-step linguistic deduction, while Detect-Then-Reason (DTR) uses region tokens to detect 3D geometric cues before quantitative inference. Training proceeds in two stages: a cold-start supervised fine-tuning phase builds chain-of-thought supervision and exposes a region-to-3D interface, followed by reinforcement learning with accuracy, format, and (for DTR) discrete center-based detection rewards. SR-REAL significantly outperforms spatial VLM baselines across diverse benchmarks, with joint training of both paths yielding mutual reinforcement and cross-dataset generalization without per-task tuning.
