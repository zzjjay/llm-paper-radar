**Diffusion** · topic 4/5 · practicality 5/5 · composite 9/10 · This work targets W8A8 INT8 quantization of diffusion transformers on consumer Ampere GPUs, proposing a fused Triton INT8 GEMM kernel that fixes the 'fake INT8' problem in production implementations (where quantization is immediately followed by dequantization to bf16 then a matmul), which prevented the hardware's INT8 tensor cores from being engaged. #### Summary
On consumer Ampere GPUs, W8A8 INT8 quantization of diffusion transformers is often slower than the FP8/NF4 alternatives it should beat. The authors trace this to a software artifact: the production "INT8" forward quantizes weights and activations only to immediately dequantize back to bf16 and run a bf16 matmul, never engaging the GPU's INT8 tensor cores. They fix this with a single fused Triton INT8 GEMM (int8xint8->int32 on Ampere tensor cores, per-token x per-channel dequant and bias folded into the epilogue, autotuned per GEMM shape) dropped into Ideogram 4.0's linear layers. The kernel is bit-exact against torch._int_mm, matches the reference at cosine similarity 1.0 with no NaNs, and runs 2.8-4.2x faster than bf16 per GEMM. End to end it gives ~1.1x at 768px and 156.5s per 1024px image on a single RTX 3090, beating NF4 (164.5s) and FP8 (172.9s) at no measurable PickScore/CLIPScore cost; the win is specific to consumer Ampere and loses to native bf16/FP8 on A100/B200.
