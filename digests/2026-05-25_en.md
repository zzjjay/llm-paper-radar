### 1. CONF-KV: Confidence-Aware KV Cache Eviction with Mixed-Precision Storage for Long-Horizon LLM (7/10)

#### 🧭 Why this paper
**KV cache compression** · topic 3/5 · practicality 4/5 · composite 7/10 · CONF-KV proposes an adaptive KV cache eviction policy driven by next-token confidence, combined with FP16/INT8 mixed-precision storage and a pyramidal per-layer budget; the scheme is simple and calibration-free. Practicality is good: the algorithm is simple (online confidence score + composite ranking), needs no calibration, and FP16/INT8 mixed precision is implementable on mainstream GPUs, but explicit end-to-end latency numbers are missing.

#### Summary
Long-horizon LLM inference makes the KV cache the dominant GPU-memory cost, yet common eviction policies rely on static recency windows or historical attention and ignore the model's per-step uncertainty. CONF-KV turns the next-token distribution into a scalar confidence score that sets the per-step cache budget (more context when uncertain, aggressive pruning when confident); inside each budget, tokens are ranked by accumulated attention mass plus recency, with a protected recent window for local coherence. It is combined with blockwise online-softmax attention, mixed FP16/INT8 storage, and a pyramidal per-layer budget. Across four model families with generations up to 4K, CONF-KV stays near a 512-token sliding-window footprint while staying within 1.5--2.1 PPL of full KV; on 32K Needle-in-a-Haystack it hits 91.4% retrieval vs 53.8% (sliding) and 80.6% (H2O), and on 75 VisualWebArena tasks it retains 95.3% of full-KV success at 2.8x lower peak memory.

- 💡 Per-step KV budget driven by next-token confidence score
- 🔧 Mixed FP16/INT8 storage with pyramidal per-layer budgets
- 📊 32K NIAH 91.4% vs H2O 80.6%, sliding 53.8%
- 📊 VWA: 95.3% of full-KV success at 2.8x lower peak memory
