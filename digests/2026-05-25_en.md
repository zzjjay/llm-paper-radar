#### Summary
Targets the KV-cache GPU-memory bottleneck of long-horizon LLM inference with CONF-KV, which converts the next-token distribution into a scalar confidence score and uses it to size the per-step cache budget—retaining more context when uncertain and pruning aggressively when confident. Within each budget, tokens are ranked by a composite of accumulated attention mass and recency, with a protected recent window for local coherence. The policy is combined with blockwise online-softmax attention, FP16/INT8 mixed-precision storage, and a pyramidal per-layer budget variant. Up to 4K generation, CONF-KV stays near a fixed 512-token sliding-window footprint while remaining within 1.5--2.1 PPL of full KV; on 32K Needle-in-a-Haystack it reaches 91.4% retrieval (vs 53.8% sliding window, 80.6% H2O), and retains 95.3% of full-KV success on 75 VisualWebArena tasks at 2.8x lower peak memory.
