#### 🧭 Why this paper
**KV cache compression** · topic 3/5 · practicality 4/5 · composite 7/10 · CONF-KV proposes a dynamic KV cache eviction policy based on next-token distribution confidence, combined with FP16/INT8 mixed-precision storage and pyramidal per-layer budgets — squarely in the kv_cache compression area. #### Summary
Targeting KV-cache pressure in long-horizon LLM inference, CONF-KV turns the next-token distribution already computed at each decoding step into a scalar confidence score and uses it to size the per-step cache budget — keeping more context when uncertain, pruning aggressively when confident. Within each budget, tokens are ranked by accumulated attention mass plus recency, with a protected recent window for local coherence; the policy is combined with blockwise online-softmax attention, mixed FP16/INT8 storage, and a pyramidal per-layer budget. Across four model families at up to 4K generated tokens, CONF-KV matches the footprint of a 512-token sliding window while staying within 1.5--2.1 PPL of full KV. On Needle-in-a-Haystack up to 32K, it hits 91.4% retrieval accuracy vs 53.8% (sliding) and 80.6% (H2O); on 75 VisualWebArena tasks it retains 95.3% of full-KV success at 2.8x lower peak memory.

- 📊 91.4% accuracy on 32K NIAH vs 80.6% H2O and 53.8% sliding window
- 📊 Retains 95.3% of full-KV success on VisualWebArena at 2.8x lower peak memory
- 🔧 Combines FP16/INT8 mixed storage with pyramidal per-layer budgets
