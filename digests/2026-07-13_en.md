#### Summary
Existing second-order PTQ methods such as GPTQ build quantization objectives solely from input activation statistics, implicitly treating all output channels as equally important. KronQ introduces gradient covariance into the quantization pipeline under a Kronecker-factored Hessian approximation, exploiting both activation and gradient covariances at two complementary levels: (1) bidirectional incoherence processing that extends input-side random rotation to the output dimension, and (2) a new inter-layer mixed-precision sensitivity metric derived from gradient and activation Hessian traces. On 2-bit weight-only quantization of LLaMA-3-70B, KronQ achieves 7.93 perplexity on WikiText-2, while GPTQ and GPTAQ diverge or produce degenerate quantizations with perplexity exceeding 2000.
