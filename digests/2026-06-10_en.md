#### Summary
Targeting consumer Ampere RTX 3090 GPUs that lack FP8 tensor cores, the paper applies PTQ to Ideogram 4.0 (a 9.3B flow-matching DiT, shipped as two separate-weight copies of a 34-layer single-stream backbone for CFG, conditioned by a Qwen3-VL-8B encoder). The INT8 W8A8 recipe (per-channel weights, per-token dynamic activations, SmoothQuant, mixed-precision protection of a small high-fragility layer set) holds the FP8 quality ceiling: on a 200-prompt benchmark the same-seed bootstrap CI for INT8-FP8 includes zero on both Pick and CLIP, while INT8 beats NF4 by +1.9 CLIP (95% CI [+1.21,+2.64]). Ablation isolates FFN down-projection protection as the dominant quality lever, and per-category OCR analysis confirms text legibility is preserved. GGUF Q4_K beats NF4 at equal on-disk size and is the quality-memory Pareto winner, but INT8 weights match FP8's footprint rather than shrink it, so an Ampere speedup awaits a fused INT8 kernel.
