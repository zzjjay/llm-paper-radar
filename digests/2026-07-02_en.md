#### Summary
In disaggregated inference, long-context workloads require transferring large KV caches across the network, blocking decoding until the transfer completes. Lynx challenges the assumption that the KV cache must be fully received before use, exploiting the observation that more significant bits capture coarse attention structure while less significant bits refine precision. It partitions the KV cache into a high-priority Anchor stream (most significant bits) and a low-priority Residual stream, starting speculative decoding immediately upon Anchor receipt while the Residual stream transfers concurrently, with a verification step ensuring equivalence to higher-precision decoding. Across multiple models and workloads, Lynx achieves TTFT comparable to aggressive 4-bit KV quantization, accuracy matching BF16, TTFT improvement over 8-bit KV quantization of up to 1.43×, and accuracy improvement over state-of-the-art of up to 5.1%.

#### Summary
Diffusion transformers face expensive inference due to multi-step sampling and large parameter counts, while existing PTQ methods require re-fitting calibration data for each new checkpoint or modality. OrbitQuant is a data-agnostic weight-activation quantizer that applies a randomized permuted block-Hadamard (RPBH) rotation to map activations into a normalized basis where each coordinate's marginal distribution is fixed regardless of timestep, prompt, or input. A single Lloyd-Max codebook therefore suffices across all timesteps, prompts, and layers of a given dimension, and the rotation is absorbed offline into the weights so only a forward rotation on activations remains at runtime. Evaluated on FLUX.1, Z-Image-Turbo, Wan 2.1, and CogVideoX, it achieves state-of-the-art PTQ at multiple low-bit settings and extends image DiT PTQ to W2A4 with usable generation quality.

- 🎯 RPBH rotation fixes the activation marginal distribution so a single Lloyd-Max codebook covers all timesteps, prompts, and layers
- 💡 Rotation absorbed offline into weights; only a forward activation rotation needed at runtime — no calibration data required
- 📊 Achieves PTQ state-of-the-art across FLUX.1, Z-Image-Turbo, Wan 2.1, and CogVideoX at several low-bit configurations
- 📊 Pushes image DiT PTQ to W2A4 while maintaining usable generation quality
