# NeurIPS 2025 — LLM Inference Optimization Trend Report

*198 in-scope papers, filtered from 5,286 NeurIPS 2025 accepts (keyword pre-filter → 471 candidates → LLM classification → 198 in-scope; see [Method](#method--distribution)). Scope follows the ICML/ICLR reports: broadened past deployable-on-vLLM systems to algorithmic inference-efficiency work. I read all abstracts in the four largest clusters (KV cache, speculative decoding, efficient attention, quantization = 123 papers) in full and characterized reasoning-efficiency, pruning, serving, early-exit, MoE and long-context from titles + a sampled read. Representative papers are linked; counts are exact, citations illustrative. Figures are authors' self-reported bests. Analysis: Claude Opus 4.8, read directly (no sub-agent fan-out).*

## Takeaway

**NeurIPS 2025 is the one venue of the four that keeps deep algorithms and real systems co-design under the same roof — it is neither MLSys (systems-only) nor ICLR (algorithms-only), and reading it is how you see the two halves of the field before they split by venue.** The same corpus that redesigns the attention operator ([linear/hybrid architectures](https://openreview.net/forum?id=WZQXaTNYEB)) also ships single-kernel distributed MoE ([FlashMoE](https://openreview.net/forum?id=EZfDHprhZM)), Hopper cluster-level fusion primitives ([ClusterFusion](https://openreview.net/forum?id=eX0m4qMYVN)), and GPU-direct SSD KV swapping ([HiFC](https://openreview.net/forum?id=onhjdWCxZY)). That breadth is the defining feature; three signals organize it:

1. **The KV cache is the largest single object (42 of 198) and is visibly mutating from a per-request scratchpad into a reusable, shareable, communicable artifact.** Precompute-and-concatenate document caches ([KVLink](https://openreview.net/forum?id=oDcAGSXZZP)), cross-agent KV reuse ([KVCOMM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=yGOytgjurF)), workflow-aware prefix caching ([KVFlow](https://openreview.net/forum?id=5Iw1nDtYmT)), and structural KV as condensed representation ([Graph-KV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=J4w4RtwLyB)). This is the *root* of the "KV as representation/communication" theme that blooms at ICLR 2026 — it is already here, one cycle earlier.

2. **Reasoning models are the workload gravity well, exactly as at ICML/ICLR.** Every cluster has a reasoning sub-thread — reasoning KV ([R-KV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=2jwAjomEDB), [Reasoning Path Compression](https://openreview.net/forum?id=894Yo61h1P)), step-level speculation ([SpecReason](https://openreview.net/forum?id=wCbOKbZ7kf), [Lookahead Reasoning](https://openreview.net/forum?id=JxxKj8pow1)), reasoning-aware attention ([Multipole](https://openreview.net/forum?id=5Qe7AGO3Eq)) — plus a dedicated 17-paper "efficient reasoning" cluster attacking overthinking directly ([VeriThinker](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Y1bt0YIS6Y), [TokenSqueeze](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Wc1VZ2bVJn), [How Far Are We From Optimal Reasoning Efficiency](https://openreview.net/forum?id=NhAi1w3s8Z)). The efficient-reasoning wave that dominates ICLR 2026 was already a first-class NeurIPS 2025 theme.

3. **The DeepSeek MLA effect is the year's clearest architectural through-line.** A cluster of work migrates existing GQA checkpoints into Multi-head Latent Attention to shrink the KV cache and plug into DeepSeek's optimized stack ([TransMLA](https://openreview.net/forum?id=TcVCu2PKb9), [Multi-head Temporal Latent Attention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=fm14gUThwh), [X-EcoMLA / Zebra-Llama](https://openreview.net/forum?id=l42UGsdrNn)) — a 2025-specific signature reacting to a specific released model.

**One cross-venue number worth flagging: quantization is conspicuously smaller here (18) than at ICLR 2026 (43) or ICML 2026 (48).** [conjecture — this spans venue and year, so it could be a NeurIPS-2025-cycle timing effect: the FP4/microscaling hardware wave (Blackwell) accelerated into the 2026 review cycle.] What quant *is* here leans toward vector-quantization/codebook and the sub-1-bit frontier ([LittleBit at 0.1 bit/weight](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zJzu9evD5K), [ParetoQ's unified 1–4-bit study](https://openreview.net/forum?id=PMSNd8xTHp)), a different flavor from ICLR's rotation/FP4 focus. The theory-under-quant pattern still holds — info-theoretic bit allocation ([Q-Palette](https://openreview.net/forum?id=l4F50jpiVH)), lattice VQ via Babai rounding ([GLVQ](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Ynwl0V1YH0)) — the same lattice framing ICLR 2026 later gives GPTQ.

**Scope honesty:** as with ICML/ICLR, "inference optimization" here includes new architectures (linear attention, hybrids, diffusion LMs) whose motivation is inference but which **require training and are not drop-in.** Read this as "what NeurIPS contributes upstream of and including deployment," not "what you can turn on tomorrow" (§8).

## 1. The KV cache — largest object, and turning into a reusable artifact (42 papers)

**The KV cache is the single most-attacked object at NeurIPS 2025, and the most important shift is that a large fraction no longer treats it as disposable per-request state but as something to reuse, share, or store across requests, agents, and even models.** Four threads run in parallel. **(a) Better eviction/importance** than attention-weight heuristics — value-vector geometry ([AnDPro anchor-direction](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Tdl89SZItB), [CurDKV CUR-decomposition leverage scores](https://openreview.net/forum?id=klmc4fwPLd)), key similarity ([KeyDiff](https://openreview.net/forum?id=uBaFH7aQnC)), learned temporal patterns ([AttentionPredictor](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Guar1tumDr)), adaptive head-wise budgets ([Ada-KV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=tcisuhGsQZ)), and context-reconstruction importance ([KVzip](https://openreview.net/forum?id=JFygzwx8SJ)). **(b) Compression by low-rank / VQ / sparsity** — latent-space projection ([SALS](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zJSZupQ889), [MTLA temporal latent](https://openreview.net/forum?id=fm14gUThwh)), tensor decomposition ([TPA / T6](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ECTxVRFhUa)), calibration-free vector quantization at 1–2 bit ([NSNQuant](https://openreview.net/forum?id=boNYskaXnO)), polar-transform key quantization ([PolarQuant](https://openreview.net/forum?id=JCTTLKEBza)), and unstructured pruning with a custom kernel ([MUSTAFAR](https://openreview.net/forum?id=C69741fMFX)). **(c) Reuse / communication (the new thread)** — concatenating independently-precomputed document caches ([KVLink](https://openreview.net/forum?id=oDcAGSXZZP)), aligning cache offsets to reuse overlapping context across agents ([KVCOMM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=yGOytgjurF)), workflow-aware prefix eviction ([KVFlow](https://openreview.net/forum?id=5Iw1nDtYmT)), semantic cross-session reuse ([SmartCache](https://openreview.net/forum?id=lMxuq0GNeC)), edge inter-task reuse ([Sim-LLM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=z1Cvcovlms)), and structural/graph KV ([Graph-KV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=J4w4RtwLyB)). **(d) Placement / offload** — GPU-direct SSD swapping ([HiFC](https://openreview.net/forum?id=onhjdWCxZY)), CPU ANNS retrieval ([RetrievalAttention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=8z3cOVER4z)), and streaming memory caps ([InfiniPot-V](https://openreview.net/forum?id=hFxOZjHyTg)). Reasoning gets its own eviction ([R-KV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=2jwAjomEDB), [Reasoning Path Compression](https://openreview.net/forum?id=894Yo61h1P), [inference-time hyper-scaling via KV compression](https://openreview.net/forum?id=8ZiElzQxf1)). Judgment: the eviction/compression thread is mature and mostly drop-in, but the reuse thread is the one to watch — it presumes an inference stack where KV is a first-class, portable object (concatenated, offset-corrected, cross-model), which no production engine fully supports yet; ICLR 2026 pushes this further, so treat NeurIPS 2025's reuse papers as the leading edge, not shipping infrastructure.

## 2. Attention itself — sparse operators and the linear/hybrid architecture push (30 papers)

**NeurIPS attacks the attention operator's quadratic cost directly, and the strongest signal is the maturation of linear/hybrid architectures from curiosities into checkpoint-competitive designs.** Three splits. **(a) Adaptive sparse attention** — nucleus-style adaptive budgets ([Twilight](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Ve693NkzcU)), self-distilled learnable gates ([SeerAttention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Nf8yfPDFTl)), correcting the distribution shift sparse attention induces ([Delta Attention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=LZrRvYBqsJ)), MoE-style block routing deployed in production ([MoBA](https://openreview.net/forum?id=RlqYCpTu1P)), and batch-scalable head sparsity ([Polar Sparsity](https://openreview.net/forum?id=cg2S1qqNSq)). **(b) Linear / sub-quadratic architectures + hybrids** — this is the deepest sub-cluster: zero-sum linear attention ([ZeroS](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Ms6IXbfzzX)), bidirectional linear ([LION](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Ar62cqTduE)), chunked RNN+attention ([RAT](https://openreview.net/forum?id=bk1IlSAwxR)), bilinear-RNN control ([Comba](https://openreview.net/forum?id=jlJaRXDzCE)), tiled linear-attention kernels beating FlashAttention ([TFLA/xLSTM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=b6H64u6TqI)), and — the headline — **NAS over attention operators that freezes a pretrained model's MLP and searches attention blocks** ([Jet-Nemotron](https://openreview.net/forum?id=WZQXaTNYEB), up to 53× decode throughput; [Nemotron-Flash](https://openreview.net/forum?id=KTDAbnFsQj)), plus decoder-hybrid-decoder memory sharing ([SambaY/GMU](https://openreview.net/forum?id=twSE0WA1vh)) and gated attention that ships in [Qwen3-Next](https://openreview.net/forum?id=1b7whO4SfY). **(c) Structured/kernel + reasoning/visual** — Monarch-matrix attention ([MonarchAttention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=XfHfTqeXfZ)), FP4 Blackwell attention ([SageAttention3](https://openreview.net/forum?id=JbJVWljk7r), 1038 TOPS), reasoning-aware clustering ([Multipole](https://openreview.net/forum?id=5Qe7AGO3Eq)), and a wave of trainable sparse attention for video diffusion ([VSA](https://openreview.net/forum?id=VrYCLQ5inI), [Radial Attention O(n log n)](https://openreview.net/forum?id=hYovE4nHTt)). A grounded reframing worth noting: [Kinetics](https://openreview.net/forum?id=phWwTrrJFv) argues test-time scaling is bottlenecked by *attention*, not parameters, making sparse attention essential to scaling. Judgment: linear/hybrid attention is the highest-ceiling bet in the set and 2025 is where it stopped being a toy — but every one of these changes the model, so the wins presuppose training or conversion; the drop-in slice is the training-free sparse-attention methods (Twilight, Delta, MonarchAttention).

## 3. Speculative & parallel decoding — EAGLE-line matures, diffusion and reasoning reshape it (33 papers)

**Speculative decoding is a crowded, maturing cluster, and its two most interesting movements are the escape from token-level equivalence toward *semantic* speculation for reasoning, and the diffusion/parallel-decoding paradigm.** The **classic draft-verify line** is well-developed — direct token prediction ([EAGLE-3](https://openreview.net/forum?id=4exx1hUffq), 6.5×), token-alignment training ([GRIFFIN](https://openreview.net/forum?id=JwnAItQF9v)), acceptance-rate-aligned distillation ([AdaSPEC](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zNLlglSOwD)), lossless leaf-to-root tree verification ([Traversal Verification](https://openreview.net/forum?id=8nOMhDFpkU)), and suffix-tree caching for repetitive agent workloads ([SuffixDecoding](https://openreview.net/forum?id=uwL0vbeEVn)). **Reasoning breaks the token-equivalence assumption**: speculate whole reasoning *steps* that need only be semantically correct ([SpecReason](https://openreview.net/forum?id=wCbOKbZ7kf), [Lookahead Reasoning](https://openreview.net/forum?id=JxxKj8pow1) — step-level parallelism that multiplies token-level SD), or route only path-divergent tokens to the big model ([R2R](https://openreview.net/forum?id=DpeJYRFRQY)). **Diffusion/parallel decoding** is a substantial thread — masked-diffusion sampling ([KLASS](https://openreview.net/forum?id=gOG9Zoyn4R)), adaptive parallel decoding inverting the draft/verify roles ([APD](https://openreview.net/forum?id=xwqTt26NJf)), encoder-decoder diffusion ([E2D2](https://openreview.net/forum?id=5jneOToPou)). **Systems/deployment**: offloaded-model substitute speculation ([SubSpec](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ZDpPfg9pDc), 12.5×), edge-assisted ([SpecEdge](https://openreview.net/forum?id=4QVLKwgg3S)), layer-parallel multi-GPU ([EasySpec](https://openreview.net/forum?id=RGUcF6pIZN)), on-device cross-vocabulary drafting ([OmniDraft](https://openreview.net/forum?id=RALtozQipi)), and MoE-specific SD ([MoESD](https://openreview.net/forum?id=FAeU7516MR)); plus SSM tree decoding ([STree](https://openreview.net/forum?id=a95Vd41o1u)) and multimodal SD ([DREAM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=M5jz47umjR), [ViSpec](https://openreview.net/forum?id=x2BsIdJJJW)). Judgment: for reasoning workloads the semantic-speculation papers (SpecReason, Lookahead, R2R) are the more consequential direction — they lift the exponential acceptance ceiling that caps token-level SD on long CoT — but they trade the lossless guarantee for "semantically equivalent," so they belong in the lossy-acceleration tier, not alongside EAGLE-3's exact-distribution methods.

## 4. Reasoning as the workload — efficient reasoning and dynamic depth (17 + 7 = 24 papers)

**The dedicated reasoning-efficiency and early-exit clusters make explicit what the other sections show implicitly: the expensive workload is the reasoning model, and its cost is largely self-inflicted overthinking.** Efficient reasoning (17) splits into **CoT compression / anti-overthinking** — verification-task fine-tuning that suppresses redundant reflection ([VeriThinker](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Y1bt0YIS6Y)), self-generated concise-CoT distillation ([TokenSqueeze](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Wc1VZ2bVJn)), perplexity-based step pruning ([LIMOPro](https://openreview.net/forum?id=W9Y0jtf45v)), hybrid long/short CoT selection ([Ada-R1](https://openreview.net/forum?id=a9MfGUHjF8)), and RL for conciseness ([Training LLMs to Reason Efficiently](https://openreview.net/forum?id=AiZxn84Wdo)) — and **adaptive test-time-compute budgeting** — budget-conditioned RL ([AnytimeReasoner](https://openreview.net/forum?id=jFaFCc5978)), calibrated-PRM instance-adaptive scaling ([Know What You Don't Know](https://openreview.net/forum?id=hzMkfIrdDT)), verification-granularity tuning ([VG-Search](https://openreview.net/forum?id=cxb5EsQHW3)), and cheaper Best-of-N ([Self-Certainty](https://openreview.net/forum?id=29FRqmVQK8)). Notably, NeurIPS also brings **diagnosis and theory** the systems venues don't: an empirical efficiency frontier + gap metric ([How Far Are We From Optimal Reasoning Efficiency](https://openreview.net/forum?id=NhAi1w3s8Z)), the "underthinking" phenomenon ([Thoughts Are All Over the Place](https://openreview.net/forum?id=WcUo7Z2Jnh)), and a theory of sampling-based scaling ([bridging internal probability and self-consistency](https://openreview.net/forum?id=E0PaeSszLz)). Early-exit / dynamic depth (7) is increasingly reasoning-shaped — adaptive reasoning formats ([ARM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=z9oeQrcNh9)), fast/slow dual-process ([Thinker](https://openreview.net/forum?id=psfk761b6H)), latent-space shortcuts ([System-1.5 Reasoning](https://openreview.net/forum?id=MNduv07wAu), 20×), recursive per-token depth with KV sharing ([Mixture-of-Recursions](https://openreview.net/forum?id=QuqsEIVWIG)) — and one paper closes the loop to serving: answer-stabilization early exit wired into a reasoning-aware scheduler ([Certaindex/Dynasor](https://openreview.net/forum?id=nn51ewu5k2), 50% compute savings). Judgment: this is the most *deployable-in-spirit* distinctive NeurIPS work (much is decoding/prompting-level), but it shares ICLR's weakness — "overthinking" is measured a dozen inconsistent ways, and the one honest attempt at a shared frontier (REG) exists precisely because the numbers otherwise don't compare; trust the direction, not the leaderboard.

## 5. Quantization — smaller here, VQ/codebook-flavored, still the theory home (18 papers)

**Quantization is a smaller cluster at NeurIPS 2025 than at ICLR/ICML 2026, and what's here tilts toward vector-quantization/codebook methods and the sub-1-bit frontier rather than the FP4/rotation work that dominates the 2026 venues.** The **sub-1-bit / extreme low-bit** thread is aggressive — 0.1 bit/weight via latent factorization + binarization ([LittleBit](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zJzu9evD5K)), 1-bit with wavelet enhancement ([HBLLM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=3FsM6wWQL4)), and a unified 1/1.58/2/3/4-bit study finding a learning transition between 2 and 3 bits ([ParetoQ](https://openreview.net/forum?id=PMSNd8xTHp)). **VQ / codebook / lattice** is the distinctive flavor — grouped lattice VQ trained with Babai rounding ([GLVQ](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Ynwl0V1YH0), the same lattice framing ICLR 2026 gives GPTQ), Riemannian sensitivity-aware VQ ([RSAVQ](https://openreview.net/forum?id=8Ounc8L4F7)), fractional-bit quantizers at the Gaussian distortion-rate bound ([Q-Palette](https://openreview.net/forum?id=l4F50jpiVH)), and a codebook-centric GEMM that skips dequantization ([CodeGEMM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=OH7U836jKk)). **Theory** holds the venue's usual role — info-theoretic bit allocation (Q-Palette), quantization-error-propagation compensation ([QEP](https://openreview.net/forum?id=a3l3K9khbL)), and unified scaling laws across compressed formats ([Unified Scaling Laws for Compressed Representations](https://openreview.net/forum?id=24wDPGiDzA)). And a **hardware/serving bridge** the pure-algorithm venues lack — lossless BF16 entropy coding ([DFloat11](https://openreview.net/forum?id=xdNAVP7TGy), 30% smaller, bit-identical), FP16/FP8 overlay for SLO-aware serving ([NestedFP](https://openreview.net/forum?id=WDAKFpWftI)), dynamic per-layer precision ([DP-LLM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ppKDXf55lY)), and adaptation to analog in-memory hardware ([Analog Foundation Models](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zo4zYTR8vn)). Judgment: don't read the smaller count as "quant is fading" — [venue artifact / timing] the pure-format frontier moved to the 2026 cycle; the durable NeurIPS contribution is the theory (distortion-rate-optimal bit allocation, lattice framing) and the serving-format bridge (DFloat11, NestedFP), which the systems venues consume rather than produce.

## 6. The systems side NeurIPS uniquely keeps — serving, MoE systems, kernels (8 + 4 + kernels)

**This section is the reason NeurIPS 2025 reads differently from ICLR 2026: it accepts genuine systems papers — kernels, schedulers, distributed operators — that a pure algorithms venue routes elsewhere.** Serving (8) spans on-chip fusion via Hopper cluster primitives ([ClusterFusion](https://openreview.net/forum?id=eX0m4qMYVN), 1.61×), online/offline SLO-aware co-location ([HyGen](https://openreview.net/forum?id=cQxLCVa9u7)), pipeline layer redistribution with async KV migration ([DynaPipe](https://openreview.net/forum?id=D6w7wIN360)), multimodal elastic parallelism ([ElasticMM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Zd6VyjmN1S)), unified LoRA fine-tune+serve ([Loquetier](https://openreview.net/forum?id=45igeoC560)), session-aware agent serving ([AgServe](https://openreview.net/forum?id=RmqWt1btxQ)), training-free online routing with a competitive-ratio guarantee ([PORT](https://openreview.net/forum?id=d4mZyZB5I9)), and TEE-based secure on-device inference ([LoRO](https://openreview.net/forum?id=de07K7kreI)). MoE (4) is systems-heavy — a fully GPU-resident single-kernel distributed MoE ([FlashMoE](https://openreview.net/forum?id=EZfDHprhZM), 9× utilization), inference-time cognitive-expert steering ([RICE](https://openreview.net/forum?id=x7fCiuCCAu)), private MoE with balanced routing ([CryptoMoE](https://openreview.net/forum?id=8pEqukyGrj)), and few-shot domain expert pruning of DeepSeek-R1 ([EASY-EP](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Vvb27TQzO9)). Judgment: these overlap MLSys in *kind*, and that's the point — NeurIPS is the only algorithms-heavy venue where a serving team can find real systems work next to the algorithms, but the overlap is shallow (8 serving papers, not a coherent agenda), so for production-grade serving the systems venues remain the primary source; NeurIPS's serving value is the analysis-plus-guarantee flavor (PORT's competitive ratio, HyGen's interference model).

## 7. Pruning, distillation, and the smaller clusters (13 + 2)

**Pruning/distillation here is dominated by two ideas: parallelizing or linearizing the transformer's own computation, and aggressive visual-token pruning for multimodal models.** The structural-efficiency thread is strong — parallelizing FFN sequences to cut depth ([FFN Fusion](https://openreview.net/forum?id=XUmGMBRv4M), Llama-405B→253B, 1.71×), group-aware pruning of hybrid SSM models ([Nemotron-H 8B→4B](https://openreview.net/forum?id=m3huAdsaGI)), global structural pruning ([Týr-the-Pruner](https://openreview.net/forum?id=rAuRLePL2R), 50% of Llama-3.1-70B), and distilling softmax into linear attention with a principled feature-dimension choice ([Degrees of Freedom for Linear Attention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=7qq1UeCYL6)). A large multimodal token-pruning sub-thread ([FastVID](https://openreview.net/forum?id=2xS4VtpApy) 90% video tokens, [CDPruner](https://openreview.net/forum?id=BLLixcuZgl), [HoloV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zKoeRtye8o), [EfficientVLA](https://openreview.net/forum?id=SELYlDHZk2)) reflects how much NeurIPS inference work is VLM/video, not text-only — flag these as non-core text-LLM. Long-context (2) is thin but architectural ([scale-invariant attention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ONE9LYBQYS) for train-short/infer-long; [prompt compression via evaluator heads](https://openreview.net/forum?id=yOs12gdsaL)). Judgment: FFN Fusion and hybrid-SSM pruning are the durable ideas here — they cut cost on the dense forward pass every serving system runs — while the visual-token-pruning papers, though numerous, are modality-specific and shouldn't be counted as general LLM-serving advances.

## 8. Maturity: practicality vs research

**NeurIPS 2025 spans the full readiness axis — the only one of the four venues to do so — which makes the drop-in-vs-training cut the single most useful filter for a reader deciding what to adopt.**

- **Deployed / production-proven** (rare but present): [MoBA](https://openreview.net/forum?id=RlqYCpTu1P) (Moonshot production long-context), gated attention in [Qwen3-Next](https://openreview.net/forum?id=1b7whO4SfY), [Certaindex/Dynasor](https://openreview.net/forum?id=nn51ewu5k2) (serving-integrated) — things you'd expect at MLSys, accepted here.
- **Training-free, drop-in** (the directly-usable slice): most KV eviction/compression (AnDPro, KeyDiff, Ada-KV, KVzip), training-free sparse attention (Twilight, Delta, MonarchAttention), lossless quant (DFloat11), lossless speculative decoding (Traversal Verification, EAGLE-3), decoding-level efficient reasoning. These cluster in KV + attention + SD + decoding — the overlap with the systems venues.
- **Not deployable without training / conversion** (the largest group): every linear/hybrid architecture (Jet-Nemotron, LION, Comba, SambaY), MLA migration (TransMLA, X-EcoMLA), QAT/sub-1-bit (LittleBit, ParetoQ), RL-based efficient reasoning, MoR. Real efficiency claims on a different cost basis.
- **Diagnosis / theory / benchmarks** (a NeurIPS strength): reasoning-efficiency frontiers ([REG](https://openreview.net/forum?id=NhAi1w3s8Z)), the [Kinetics scaling law](https://openreview.net/forum?id=phWwTrrJFv), unified compression scaling laws, sampling-based-scaling theory — the "why and what's the limit" the systems venues can't supply.

Takeaway: the highest-ceiling ideas (linear/hybrid attention, KV-as-artifact, semantic speculation) and the deepest theory are here; the directly-usable subset is the training-free KV/attention/SD/decoding slice; the rest is a bet on a training run or a future inference stack.

## Method & distribution

**Selection.** NeurIPS 2025 had 5,286 accepted papers on OpenReview (venue `NeurIPS.cc/2025/Conference` — note the `.cc` host; `.org` returns nothing). A recall-oriented keyword pre-filter (inference/serving/KV/attention/quantization/MoE/decoding/reasoning-efficiency/long-context/low-bit terms) cut this to 471 candidates; an LLM classifier (Claude Sonnet, relaxed inference-optimization scope) judged 198 in-scope. Same mega-venue funnel as the ICML/ICLR reports.

**Distribution of the 198 (classifier subfields):**

| Cluster | # | Cluster | # |
|---|---|---|---|
| KV cache | 42 | early-exit / dynamic depth | 7 |
| speculative / parallel decoding | 33 | MoE inference | 4 |
| efficient attention | 30 | long-context | 2 |
| quantization | 18 | (misc "other") | 24 |
| reasoning efficiency | 17 | | |
| pruning / distillation | 13 | serving systems | 8 |

**Biases, stated plainly.** (1) The keyword pre-filter misses papers whose inference angle isn't in the title/abstract, so 198 is a lower bound. (2) Scope was **relaxed** to algorithmic/architectural work, so much of the 198 needs training — see §8. (3) A large share of the pruning and KV clusters is **multimodal/video (VLM/VLA/VAR), not text-only LLM** — flagged in §1/§7, not silently counted as text-LLM serving. (4) Cross-venue comparisons to MLSys/ASPLOS/ICML/ICLR 2026 reflect venue selection *and* a one-year offset (NeurIPS 2025 vs the 2026 venues), so any "smaller/larger than" claim (e.g. quantization) confounds venue and year — tagged [conjecture] where it matters. (5) I read the four largest clusters (KV, speculative decoding, efficient attention, quantization = 123 papers) in full and characterized the rest from titles + a sampled read; per-paper claims outside the top four are lower-confidence.
