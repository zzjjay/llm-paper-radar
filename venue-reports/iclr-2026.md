# ICLR 2026 — LLM Inference Optimization Trend Report

*~300 in-scope papers, filtered from 5,351 ICLR 2026 accepts (keyword pre-filter → 606 candidates → LLM classification; ~half the 606 are out-of-domain false positives — brain-decoding, robotics, medical imaging — leaving ~300 genuine inference-efficiency papers; see [Method](#method--distribution)). Scope follows the ICML report: **broadened** past deployable-on-vLLM systems to algorithmic inference-efficiency work, otherwise ICLR's contribution vanishes. I read all abstracts in the four largest systems-adjacent clusters (KV cache, speculative decoding, efficient attention, quantization = 155 papers) in full, and characterized efficient-reasoning, serving, MoE, pruning and early-exit from titles + a sampled read. Representative papers are linked; with a corpus this size the report does **not** link all of them — counts are approximate where noted, citations are illustrative. Figures are authors' self-reported bests. Analysis: Claude Opus 4.8, read directly (no sub-agent fan-out).*

## Takeaway

**ICLR is the most algorithmic and least systems-oriented of the four 2026 venues — and its distinctive contribution is that it treats the *reasoning model* as the workload to optimize, not the server.** Where MLSys/ASPLOS optimize a fixed model on a fixed engine and ICML redesigns the model, ICLR asks a question the systems venues barely touch: given that reasoning models are now the expensive workload, how do you make them *generate less and think smarter*? Three signals separate it from the other three reports, and all three are load-bearing:

1. **"Efficient reasoning" is ICLR's signature theme — and it's the single largest cluster once you count it.** A large body of work attacks inference cost at the *token-generation* level: trim redundant reflection ([Stop Unnecessary Reflection](https://openreview.net/forum?id=aRzEtK9Ite), [TrimR](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ofEkphaqg7)), compress the chain-of-thought ([Making Slow Thinking Faster via step entropy](https://openreview.net/forum?id=cGLqQfS5wH)), budget test-time compute per-query ([Plan and Budget](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ctspw4CqbS), [Mixture-of-Length reasoning](https://openreview.net/forum?id=oWWAeLEdE3)), or reuse prior thoughts ([Retrieval-of-Thought](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Wy7NyScKlD)). This is the *algorithmic* answer to the same reasoning-cost problem that MLSys answers with serving and disaggregation — and it's placed a full venue-cycle upstream of the systems work that would deploy it.

2. **The KV cache stops being a cache and becomes a first-class representation.** The freshest idea in the whole set: KV state as something you compute once, store, share, and *communicate over* — direct model-to-model semantic transfer via KV ([Cache-to-Cache](https://openreview.net/forum?id=LeatkxrBCi)), inter-LLM KV sharing ([KVComm](https://openreview.net/forum?id=F7rUng23nw)), offline-trained "[Cartridges](https://openreview.net/forum?id=0k5w8O0SNg)" as reusable long-context, KV as reasoning substrate ([KaVa latent reasoning](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ePrhcLbtGv), [Log-Augmented Generation](https://openreview.net/forum?id=oQ3d4O8BaX)). This reframing does not appear at MLSys/ASPLOS/ICML in this form.

3. **It pushes theory under heuristics even harder than ICML.** GPTQ is proven identical to [Babai's nearest-plane algorithm on a lattice](https://openreview.net/forum?id=NFB4QGGS65); FP4's compression limit is derived from an [exponent-concentration law (~FP4.67)](https://openreview.net/forum?id=XI1CeufywD); the microscaling "smaller block is better" assumption is shown to [break down theoretically](https://openreview.net/forum?id=3jDSqfTSrn); sparse attention gets [(ε,δ) approximation guarantees](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zzTDulLys0).

**Two things confirm the cross-venue reads from the other reports.** The **diffusion-LM surge** ICML flagged is here in full force — diffusion/parallel decoding pervades the speculative-decoding, KV, and attention clusters ([Fast-dLLM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=3Z3Is6hnOT), [SparseD](https://openreview.net/forum?id=dwbrZtYP04), [Quant-dLLM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=HD7tuVakmR)) — so this is a two-venue signal now, not an ICML quirk. And the MLSys report's **"quantization faded is a venue artifact"** conjecture is confirmed a second time: 43 quantization papers here, deeper into theory and sub-4-bit than any systems venue, none of which would appear at MLSys.

**The transversal fact tying it together:** *reasoning models are the gravity well.* Every major cluster has a reasoning-specific sub-thread — reasoning KV ([ThinKV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=M3CeHnZKNC), [PM-KVQ](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Vem6FQvRvq), [LouisKV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=6RJ8fZwm4P)), reasoning quantization ([ParoQuant](https://openreview.net/forum?id=1USeVjsKau), [When Reasoning Meets Compression](https://openreview.net/forum?id=2za3iNkwXn)), reasoning early-exit ([Dynamic Early Exit in Reasoning Models](https://openreview.net/forum?id=NpU7ZXafRi)). Long chains of thought are simultaneously the reason inference got expensive and the structure everyone is exploiting to make it cheap.

**Scope honesty:** as with ICML, "inference optimization" here bleeds into "efficient architecture" — linear-attention and diffusion-LM papers are new model designs whose motivation is inference but which **require training and are not drop-in**. Read this as "what ICLR contributes upstream of deployment," not "what you can turn on tomorrow." (§8)

## 1. Efficient reasoning — ICLR's signature theme (~50–80 papers, mostly in "other")

**This is the cluster that makes ICLR different, and it's easy to miss because it's scattered across the "other" bucket rather than sitting under a tidy systems label.** The premise: with reasoning models, most inference cost is *self-inflicted* — the model over-thinks — so the highest-leverage optimization is generating fewer, better tokens. The work splits into: (a) **reflection/CoT trimming** — training or prompting models to stop over-reflecting ([Stop Unnecessary Reflection](https://openreview.net/forum?id=aRzEtK9Ite), [REA-RL reflection-aware RL](https://openreview.net/forum?id=E6keG5QDct), [CyclicReflex](https://openreview.net/forum?id=4oOF4J2xSy)) and compressing chains by step-entropy ([Making Slow Thinking Faster](https://openreview.net/forum?id=cGLqQfS5wH), [TrimR verifier-based trimming](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ofEkphaqg7)); (b) **adaptive test-time-compute budgeting** — spend tokens proportional to difficulty ([Plan and Budget](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ctspw4CqbS), [Mixture-of-Length reasoning](https://openreview.net/forum?id=oWWAeLEdE3), [Strategic Scaling via bandits](https://openreview.net/forum?id=0mNnINd2z5), [Deep Think with Confidence](https://openreview.net/forum?id=8LqHs0KIM7)); (c) **latent / non-verbal reasoning** — move reasoning off the token stream entirely ([latent reasoning via compressed KV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ePrhcLbtGv), [continuous CoT for parallel exploration](https://openreview.net/forum?id=sTPKDKn5ig)); (d) **thought reuse** ([Retrieval-of-Thought](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Wy7NyScKlD)). Judgment: this is the most *deployable-in-spirit* of ICLR's distinctive work — many methods are training-free decoding/prompting changes — but it's also the least benchmark-standardized: "over-thinking" is measured a dozen inconsistent ways, and gains are almost all self-reported on math/code with no shared harness. Treat the direction as real and the numbers as not-yet-comparable.

## 2. The KV cache — from cache to first-class representation (43 papers)

**Same villain as every venue (KV memory/bandwidth dominates long decode), but ICLR's distinctive move is to stop treating KV as a byproduct and start treating it as a computed, storable, transferable object.** This is the freshest idea in the report. Beyond ordinary compression, a genuinely new sub-cluster treats KV as a **medium**: direct semantic communication between models by passing KV instead of text ([Cache-to-Cache](https://openreview.net/forum?id=LeatkxrBCi)), selective inter-LLM KV sharing ([KVComm](https://openreview.net/forum?id=F7rUng23nw)), offline-trained reusable long-context "[Cartridges](https://openreview.net/forum?id=0k5w8O0SNg)," and KV as reusable reasoning memory ([Log-Augmented Generation](https://openreview.net/forum?id=oQ3d4O8BaX)). The **reasoning-KV** sub-thread is large — long-CoT-specific eviction/quantization ([ThinKV thought-adaptive](https://openreview.net/forum?id=M3CeHnZKNC), [PM-KVQ progressive mixed-precision for long-CoT](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Vem6FQvRvq), [LouisKV for long input-output](https://openreview.net/forum?id=6RJ8fZwm4P)). The rest tracks ICML's principled-eviction line — worst-case-risk eviction ([DefensiveKV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=nJgS06sX3O)) — plus cross-layer/low-rank ([CARE: GQA→MLA](https://openreview.net/forum?id=DVurf4kGag), [Multi-Head Low-Rank Attention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=vBJKZ19XGY)), frequency-domain compression ([FreqKV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=wFSOtyvQ9d)), and diffusion-LM caching ([Fast-dLLM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=3Z3Is6hnOT)). Judgment: the "KV as representation/communication" line is the highest-novelty, lowest-maturity bet in the set — it implies an inference stack where KV is a portable artifact across requests and even models, which nothing in production supports today; the reasoning-KV and low-rank work is the near-term-usable slice.

## 3. Speculative & parallel decoding — diffusion surge meets provable multi-draft (40 papers)

**Two forces share this cluster: the diffusion/parallel-decoding paradigm (as at ICML) and a distinctly ICLR habit of putting optimality proofs under speculative sampling.** The **theory** thread is what the systems venues don't produce: optimal multi-draft sampling via [convex optimization](https://openreview.net/forum?id=gpsczXOsHn), [no-regret drafter selection](https://openreview.net/forum?id=JMmljf895g), and [lossless hierarchical SD for joint intractability](https://openreview.net/forum?id=LaVrNaBNwM) — speculative decoding recast as a provable statistical problem, not a systems trick. The **diffusion** thread reframes decode latency as a modeling question (parallel denoising), overlapping heavily with §2/§4. The classic EAGLE-line continues — notably [PARD](https://openreview.net/forum?id=XbOyv7iVGL) (low-cost parallel-draft adaptation, from AMD). And speculation is escaping decoding itself: [Speculative Actions](https://openreview.net/forum?id=P0GOk5wslg) applies the draft-verify pattern to agent action sequences. Judgment: the lossless/provable methods are the trustworthy ones — they carry acceptance guarantees rather than benchmark wins — but "lossless X% speedup" figures still assume idealized verification and rarely survive a real serving engine unchanged (the same caveat MLSys's "Performance or Illusion?" and ICML's SPEED-Bench raised; ICLR has no equivalent audit paper, a gap).

## 4. Efficient attention — linear/sub-quadratic architectures and trainable sparsity (39 papers)

**Like ICML, ICLR attacks the attention operator's cost directly rather than scheduling around it — but ICLR leans harder into new linear-time architectures and into sparse attention you *train* rather than bolt on.** Three splits: (a) **linear/sub-quadratic architectures** as first-class models — [Mamba-3](https://openreview.net/forum?id=HwCvaJOiCj) (inference-first SSM design), [Log-Linear Attention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=mOJgZWkXKW) (logarithmically-growing state), and a unifying theory of [Prefix-Scannable Models](https://openreview.net/forum?id=tuLF84azND) that generalizes SSMs and linear transformers; (b) **trainable/native sparse attention** — the NSA line matures with better kernels ([FSA](https://openreview.net/forum?id=c5mdo1hWrs)), seamless short-to-long adaptation ([InfLLM-V2](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ZzF9V0H6Vi)), and video extension ([VideoNSA](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zA2LbsUMDd)); (c) **guaranteed / diffusion sparse attention** — sparse attention with [(ε,δ) accuracy guarantees](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zzTDulLys0) and diffusion-LM-specific sparsity ([SparseD](https://openreview.net/forum?id=dwbrZtYP04), [DPad suffix dropout](https://openreview.net/forum?id=0yOsSMU1eY)). MLA optimization also shows up at the kernel level ([TyphoonMLA mixed naive-absorb](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ZfCCwJ4Wcs)). Judgment: linear attention remains the highest-ceiling / highest-risk inference bet — it changes the model, can't be dropped into a served checkpoint, and most results are at small-to-mid scale; the trainable-sparse (NSA-family) work is more likely to reach production because it retains the transformer and ships kernels.

## 5. Quantization — deepest theory, FP4 format wars, venue-artifact confirmed (43 papers)

**This cluster is the second empirical refutation of "quantization faded," and it's more theory-driven than any systems venue's.** Three threads. (a) **Theory** — the standout is GPTQ proven identical to [Babai's nearest-plane algorithm on a Hessian lattice](https://openreview.net/forum?id=NFB4QGGS65), importing decades of lattice-algorithm results; plus an optimization-framework PTQ ([Qronos](https://openreview.net/forum?id=7axclBCYul)), an [exponent-concentration law fixing the lossless FP limit near FP4.67](https://openreview.net/forum?id=XI1CeufywD), and a theory of why [finer microscaling blocks paradoxically hurt](https://openreview.net/forum?id=3jDSqfTSrn). (b) **FP4 / microscaling format wars** — the same MX/NVFP4 hardware ASPLOS targets, argued at the format level: [MR-GPTQ closes the MXFP4→NVFP4 accuracy gap](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zCBGe9AqJZ), [MicroMix mixes MXFP4/6/8 for Blackwell](https://openreview.net/forum?id=P5OKoZdwlB). (c) **Reasoning-model quant** as its own concern ([ParoQuant for long-CoT outlier accumulation](https://openreview.net/forum?id=1USeVjsKau), [mechanistic study of which weights matter for reasoning](https://openreview.net/forum?id=2za3iNkwXn)). Plus a ternary/binary revival, joint quant+sparse ([OBR](https://openreview.net/forum?id=VQIvBpL5ag)), and diffusion-LM quant ([Quant-dLLM 2-bit](https://openreview.net/forum?id=HD7tuVakmR)). Judgment: the cross-venue division of labor is now unambiguous across three reports — ICLR/ICML own the *format and algorithm* frontier (and increasingly its theory), the systems venues own the *kernel and chip*; MLSys's "venue artifact" tag on quantization was correct both times.

## 6. The smaller clusters: MoE, pruning, early-exit, long-context (47 papers)

**These track ICML's algorithmic framing but at smaller scale — MoE-as-routing-design, architectural alternatives to the KV cache, and reasoning-aware dynamic depth.** MoE (14) treats routing as the object of study — load balancing ([Libra](https://openreview.net/forum?id=WhxNwgGkAS)), straggler mitigation ([Capacity-Aware Inference](https://openreview.net/forum?id=LuYFpySWA2)), IO/tile-aware kernels ([SonicMoE](https://openreview.net/forum?id=KzTJ1raEgB)), and the observation that a few "[super experts](https://openreview.net/forum?id=JYwGNKfPPp)" dominate. Pruning/distillation (20) is standard structured sparsity with a hardware bent — strict N:M sparsity ([MaskPro](https://openreview.net/forum?id=0R06BghLJX)), semi-structured via matrix factorization ([ARMOR](https://openreview.net/forum?id=8NE554wv0m)). Early-exit / dynamic depth (8) is increasingly **reasoning-aware** — exit when the reasoning has converged ([Dynamic Early Exit in Reasoning Models](https://openreview.net/forum?id=NpU7ZXafRi), [learned residual skip gates](https://openreview.net/forum?id=aiP6XfaYZR)) — and diffusion-aware ([stopping computation for converged tokens in masked-diffusion decoding](https://openreview.net/forum?id=PzhNnMepgl)). Long-context (5) again favors *architectural* answers over KV compression ([Markovian Thinker: architecture-agnostic linear scaling of reasoning](https://openreview.net/forum?id=3As6AQ9ELI)). Judgment: nothing here is a surprise relative to ICML, but the consistent reasoning-awareness across even these small clusters is the tell — reasoning has reshaped every inference subfield, not just the big ones.

## 7. Serving — conspicuously thin, and that's the finding (10 papers)

**The most telling number in the report is how *few* serving papers ICLR accepts (10, vs ICML's 34 and MLSys's systems core), and how scattered they are — this is the empirical proof that ICLR is not a systems venue.** There's no coherent serving agenda; instead isolated corners: kernel programmability ([TileLang](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Jb1WkNSfUB), 5–6× over Triton, AMD-GPU results), verified semantic caching ([vCache](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zF0A0xw3HZ)), agent-serving efficiency ([SearchAgent-X](https://openreview.net/forum?id=BtWBi17eVi)), heterogeneous on-device ([prima.cpp: 30–70B on home clusters](https://openreview.net/forum?id=h0LjpOG1jq)), cascade routing ([Cascadia](https://openreview.net/forum?id=wkrWbrHTJQ)), and KV cache-affinity scheduling ([DualMap](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zCadrJ32Xn)). The one genuinely useful systems contribution for practitioners is empirical: an [inference-serving study of reasoning models](https://openreview.net/forum?id=6CGjZYp6ft) showing weight/KV quant and speculative decoding help RLLM serving but prefix caching can *hurt* small RLLMs. Judgment: for anything serving-related, trust MLSys/ASPLOS — ICLR's serving papers are either niche (on-device, agents) or analytical, and lack the production-trace validation the systems venues demand.

## 8. Maturity: practicality vs research

**ICLR is the furthest from deployment of the four venues — even more than ICML — because its distinctive contributions (efficient-reasoning training, KV-as-representation, linear attention) are overwhelmingly training-dependent or infrastructure-not-yet-existing.**

- **Not deployable without training or new infra** (the largest group): every linear-attention architecture, most diffusion-LM work, the KV-as-communication line (needs an inference stack that passes KV across requests/models), efficient-reasoning methods that require RL/fine-tuning, QAT, MoE-routing changes. Real inference-efficiency claims, different cost basis than "turn it on."
- **Training-free, drop-in** (the deployable slice): most KV-eviction and PTQ methods, training-free sparse attention with guarantees ([vAttention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zzTDulLys0)), decoding-level efficient-reasoning (verifier-based trimming, confidence-based early stop), lossless speculative decoding. This is the slice a serving team can use, and it clusters in KV + PTQ + decoding — the overlap with the systems venues.
- **Theory / audits** (a strength here): the GPTQ-lattice equivalence, the FP4.67 limit, the microscaling-block-size theory, the (ε,δ) sparse-attention guarantees — ICLR's comparative advantage is telling you *why* something works and *what the limit is*, which is exactly what a systems team can't get from MLSys.

Takeaway: the highest-ceiling *ideas* and the *why-does-it-work* results are here; the directly-usable subset is the training-free KV/PTQ/decoding slice; everything else — including ICLR's most novel contributions — is a bet on a future training run or a future inference stack.

## Method & distribution

**Selection.** ICLR 2026 had ~5,351 accepted papers on OpenReview (of ~19,800 submissions). A recall-oriented keyword pre-filter (inference/serving/KV/attention/quantization/MoE/decoding/reasoning-efficiency/long-context terms) cut this to 606 candidates; an LLM classifier (Claude Sonnet, relaxed inference-optimization scope) assigned subfields. Roughly half the 606 are out-of-domain false positives — the keyword "efficient/inference/decoding/sparse" matched papers in brain-computer interfaces, robotics/VLA, medical imaging, 3D vision, protein modeling, etc. — leaving **~300 genuine inference-efficiency papers**. This two-stage funnel is the mega-venue adaptation of the venue-trend pipeline (same as the ICML report).

**Distribution of the core clusters (classifier subfields):**

| Cluster | # | Cluster | # |
|---|---|---|---|
| efficient reasoning (in "other") | ~50–80 | MoE inference | 14 |
| quantization | 43 | serving systems | 10 |
| KV cache | 43 | early-exit / dynamic depth | 8 |
| speculative / parallel decoding | 40 | long-context / memory | 5 |
| efficient attention | 39 | pruning / distillation | 20 |

**Biases, stated plainly.** (1) The keyword pre-filter misses papers whose inference angle isn't in the title/abstract keywords — recall is imperfect, so ~300 is a lower bound. (2) The **efficient-reasoning count is genuinely fuzzy**: it's spread across the "other" bucket and its size depends on where you draw the line between reasoning-*efficiency* and reasoning-*quality* — I report a range, not a point estimate, on purpose. (3) Scope was **relaxed** to include algorithmic/architectural work, so much of the ~300 is not deployable without training — see §8. (4) ICLR is a representation-learning venue, so this is "what ICLR accepted," heavily upstream of deployment; cross-venue comparisons to MLSys 2026 / ASPLOS 2026 / ICML 2026 are grounded in all four reports but reflect venue selection, not a field-wide count. (5) I read the four largest systems-adjacent clusters (KV, speculative decoding, efficient attention, quantization = 155 papers) in full and characterized the rest from titles + a sampled read — counts are approximate where marked, and per-paper claims outside the top four clusters are lower-confidence.
