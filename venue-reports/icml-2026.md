# ICML 2026 — LLM Inference Optimization Trend Report

*366 in-scope papers, filtered from 6,341 ICML 2026 accepts (keyword pre-filter → LLM classification of 762 candidates → 366 judged in-scope; see [Method](#method--distribution)). Scope was deliberately **broadened** past deployable-on-vLLM systems to include algorithmic inference-efficiency work — otherwise ICML's actual contribution vanishes. Of the 366, I read all abstracts in the four largest clusters (speculative decoding, KV cache, quantization, efficient attention = 202 papers) in full and characterized the rest from titles + a sampled read; representative and standout papers are linked, but with a corpus this size the report **does not link all 366** — counts are exact, citations are illustrative. Figures are authors' self-reported bests. Analysis: Claude Opus 4.8, read directly (no sub-agent fan-out).*

## Takeaway

**ICML is the algorithmic engine room of LLM inference, and its scale says so: ~366 in-scope papers — roughly 6× MLSys 2026 (57) and 12× ASPLOS 2026 (29).** The systems venues optimize *around* a fixed model on a fixed engine; ICML changes the model, the number format, the attention operator, and even the generation paradigm. Two things separate it from the systems venues, and both are load-bearing for anyone reading all three reports:

1. **It puts theory under what systems venues do by heuristic.** KV eviction gets an Optimal-Brain-Damage / information-bottleneck grounding ([OBCache](https://openreview.net/forum?id=f58VZpG6ls), [a unified information-theoretic objective](https://openreview.net/forum?id=2fPbFUJ87k)); quantization gets SQNR decomposition and information-geometry ([Dissecting Quantization Error](https://openreview.net/forum?id=7BoFyTPqMr), [SHARP-Q](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Xn9Eu2x7Hw)), a [QAT scaling law](https://openreview.net/forum?id=fXr3uPr1G5), and an [INT-vs-FP format study](https://openreview.net/forum?id=1GIYHWO9S5); serving gets a [queueing-theoretic stability analysis](https://openreview.net/forum?id=7X91AAKL5B) and [online-LP routing](https://openreview.net/forum?id=a51bLzOy1u); linear attention gets a [provable expressiveness hierarchy](https://openreview.net/forum?id=JPnA2BI6U5). If MLSys/ASPLOS answer "does it run faster," ICML answers "why, and what's the limit."

2. **It owns the paradigm bets the systems venues only consume.** Diffusion / non-autoregressive decoding and linear / sub-quadratic attention are *born* here; MLSys and ASPLOS each had one or two diffusion-drafter papers, ICML has dozens.

**The single biggest signal is the diffusion-LM surge.** Diffusion / parallel-decoding language models are no longer a curiosity — they pervade the speculative-decoding cluster (the majority of it), reappear in KV caching ([dLLM-Cache](https://openreview.net/forum?id=guLrgDb2jB)), attention ([DiffuMamba](https://openreview.net/forum?id=hLizFuTBsA)), serving ([Mosaic](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ky9RAeT6ie)), MoE, and quantization. This is a genuine paradigm contest with autoregression, and its motivation is squarely *inference* (parallel token generation). It barely registered at the 2026 systems venues — a leading indicator worth watching: the algorithmic bet is being placed a full venue-cycle before the systems work to serve it exists.

**A venue-routing fact, now empirically confirmed across all three reports:** the MLSys report conjectured that standalone weight quantization "faded" only because pure-algorithm quant is submitted elsewhere. Here it is — 48 quantization papers, deep into 2-bit / ternary / 1-bit / **sub-1-bit** territory, plus a whole linear-attention literature — none of which appears at MLSys. "The field moved on from quantization" was false; it moved *venues*.

**Scope honesty (this is the one caveat that matters):** because scope was broadened to algorithms, "inference optimization" here bleeds into "efficient architecture." Many linear-attention, MoE-routing, and diffusion-LM papers are new *model designs* whose motivation is inference efficiency but which **require training and are not drop-in** — the opposite of the MLSys/ASPLOS "plugs into vLLM" default. Read this report as "what ICML contributes upstream of deployment," not "what you can turn on tomorrow."

## 1. Speculative & parallel decoding — and the diffusion-LM surge (59 papers)

The headline isn't faster draft-and-verify; it's that **diffusion / non-autoregressive generation has become the dominant way ICML attacks decode latency**, reframing "speculative decoding" from a systems trick into a modeling-paradigm question. Roughly half of this cluster is diffusion-LM decoding: making dLLMs actually beat optimized AR engines ([WeDLM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=QwtmbKAOZU) restores causal-attention/KV-cache compatibility; [d3LLM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=rzBAQT2Fkg), [Efficient-DLM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ynRnOqI2W8) AR→dLM conversion), or turning a block-diffusion drafter into a speculative engine ([DFlash](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Oz335dV48X), 6× lossless, beating EAGLE-3; [Trajectory-Level Speculative Decoding](https://openreview.net/forum?id=pMY15tVUTT)). The classic AR-drafter line continues in parallel — draft-training objectives beyond KL ([LK losses], acceptance-rate-direct), online/RL-adapted drafters ([When RL Meets Adaptive Speculative Training](https://openreview.net/forum?id=1U0lhpW0VL)), open training infra ([SpecForge](https://openreview.net/forum?id=CQOEbxy0tE)) — and speculative decoding is spreading to image/video AR generation ([Speculative Coupled Decoding](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ea4sx1kxDz)). A telling maturity marker: ICML now has a *benchmark* auditing whether SD gains survive real engines ([SPEED-Bench](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Rl2uQlCoQX)) — the same reality-check instinct MLSys's "Performance or Illusion?" showed, arriving here too.

## 2. The KV cache — where ICML puts theory under the systems heuristics (49 papers)

Same villain as MLSys (KV memory/bandwidth dominates long decode), but ICML's contribution is **principled importance instead of heuristics**: eviction grounded in Optimal Brain Damage ([OBCache](https://openreview.net/forum?id=f58VZpG6ls)), the information bottleneck ([unified information-theoretic objective](https://openreview.net/forum?id=2fPbFUJ87k)), or learned/RL future-utility ([Learning to Evict](https://openreview.net/forum?id=0OevIlRMYN), [ForesightKV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=znV8JHv8b8)) — a direct answer to the "four incompatible heuristic stances" the MLSys report flagged as an unresolved gap. The second driver is **reasoning models**: a large sub-cluster targets long-CoT KV specifically (thought-revisiting tokens in [BeaconKV], tree-search KV in ArborKV, reasoning-head discovery), confirming reasoning as the workload gravity well across venues. Beyond eviction: cross-layer / low-rank compression ([xKV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=XDb69lykA8), [STAR-KV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=lJjH1q6RwY)), sub-1-bit vector quantization of KV ([GSRQ](https://openreview.net/forum?id=HoRGEhSUdN), [PatternKV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ozV2cpEgnZ)), cross-request/agent reuse ([RelayCaching](https://openreview.net/forum?id=1tbhBSXcyX)), GPU-native retrieval ([ParisKV](https://openreview.net/forum?id=wxD4wTYQXt)), and — again — diffusion-LM caching ([dLLM-Cache](https://openreview.net/forum?id=guLrgDb2jB)). The judgment for a practitioner: if you want the *best* KV eviction rule rather than a fast-enough one, the principled scorers here beat the recency/attention-weight heuristics that ship in serving engines — but most add per-token compute the engines were designed to avoid.

## 3. Quantization — alive, deep, and sub-1-bit (the opposite of MLSys) (48 papers)

**This cluster is the empirical refutation of "quantization faded":** 48 papers, and the frontier is well below 4-bit — ternary ([CAT-Q](https://openreview.net/forum?id=9uZJLXt7fq), matching BitNet with 100,000× fewer training tokens), 1-bit ([1-Bit Wonder](https://openreview.net/forum?id=6EGpWhH2yx)), and sub-1-bit ([LittleBit-2](https://openreview.net/forum?id=9TMdlXCn79)). Three threads: (a) **outlier handling** via function-preserving transforms ([FPTQuant](https://openreview.net/forum?id=2jpCo5lnSZ), rotations/permutations/Sinkhorn — [SINQ](https://openreview.net/forum?id=XQjgmQ8P1n)); (b) **theory** — an SQNR concentration-alignment account of why transforms work ([Dissecting Quantization Error](https://openreview.net/forum?id=7BoFyTPqMr)), Fisher-geometry PTQ ([SHARP-Q](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Xn9Eu2x7Hw)), a [QAT scaling law](https://openreview.net/forum?id=fXr3uPr1G5), and an [INT-vs-FP format study](https://openreview.net/forum?id=1GIYHWO9S5) that argues *against* the hardware trend toward FP4 at fine granularity; (c) **format co-design** with the same MX/NVFP4 hardware ASPLOS targets ([MixFP4](https://openreview.net/forum?id=dSjVTpQyIk)) — the two venues meet here, but ICML asks "which format is information-theoretically better," ASPLOS asks "what datapath runs it." Reasoning-model quant is its own thread ([ReQAT](https://openreview.net/forum?id=aKY52fnzgc): FP4 fails on low-entropy digits/operators, recovering BF16 reasoning accuracy). The cross-venue read: ICML owns the *format and algorithm* frontier, the systems venues own the *kernel and chip* — and the MLSys report's "venue artifact" tag on quantization was exactly right.

## 4. Attention itself — redesign the operator, don't just schedule it (46 papers)

**Where MLSys/ASPLOS take attention as fixed and optimize memory/kernels around it, ICML attacks the operator's cost directly** — this is the most structurally ICML-specific cluster, and it splits three ways. (a) **Linear / sub-quadratic attention** as a first-class architecture: near-linear with theory ([WildCat](https://openreview.net/forum?id=lfqyLp4hZm)), delta/Kalman/Bayesian-filter recurrences ([Kalman Linear Attention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=9h7sSJe4jN), [Dynamic Linear Attention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=RH7Ft0pVDP)), Mamba-backboned diffusion LMs ([DiffuMamba](https://openreview.net/forum?id=hLizFuTBsA)), and the striking result that [test-time training with KV binding is secretly linear attention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=MpBeXMurIb) — plus a [provable expressiveness hierarchy](https://openreview.net/forum?id=JPnA2BI6U5) for hybrid linear-full. (b) **Sparse attention** with an efficiency/hardware bent ([TileSparse](https://openreview.net/forum?id=lK2AzLDJal) arithmetic-intensity-aware, [Faster Than Flash](https://openreview.net/forum?id=r8YhlMRUR2)). (c) **Mechanistic theory** that feeds everything else: the origin of attention sinks and massive activations ([Anatomy of Massive Activations](https://openreview.net/forum?id=VUiMZBfHBe), [Structural Origin of Attention Sink](https://openreview.net/forum?id=dAWFE1gPmJ)) — the "why outliers exist" explanations that quantization and KV papers cite. The judgment: linear attention is the highest-ceiling, highest-risk inference bet in the whole set — it changes the model, so it can't be dropped into a served checkpoint, and its wins are still mostly demonstrated at small-to-mid scale.

## 5. Serving — even ICML's systems lean analytical (34 papers)

**ICML does have a real serving cluster, but its comparative advantage shows even here: it brings analysis where MLSys brings engineering.** A [queueing-theoretic stability framework](https://openreview.net/forum?id=7X91AAKL5B) for KV-constrained inference and [online-LP multi-objective routing](https://openreview.net/forum?id=a51bLzOy1u) are things you'd find at MLSys only as heuristics. The familiar systems themes recur — prefill/decode multiplexing and disaggregation ([DuetServe](https://openreview.net/forum?id=bu2Pcce6Rq) adaptive GPU multiplexing, [HexGen-3](https://openreview.net/forum?id=fVG57FKBNp), [Not All Prefills Are Equal](https://openreview.net/forum?id=RW23qIUb5f)), output-length-prediction scheduling ([uncertainty-aware](https://openreview.net/forum?id=I5IMkvVKd7)), GPU harvesting ([ConServe](https://openreview.net/forum?id=eKfWG67mZB)) — and two forward-looking angles: LLM-driven kernel optimization ([KernelBand](https://openreview.net/forum?id=d8e2jBfMpE), bandits over kernels — the same "LLMs write kernels" bet as ASPLOS's FlashInfer-Bench) and **agentic serving** (workflow-aware batching/scheduling for multi-agent systems). Diffusion-LM serving appears here too ([Mosaic](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ky9RAeT6ie), 30× context for dLLMs). Judgment: the overlap with MLSys is real but shallow — a serving idea that needs production-trace validation is better trusted from the systems venues; ICML's serving value is the analytical framing, not the deployment evidence.

## 6. MoE — a routing/algorithm design space, not a serving-tax (27 papers)

**Where MLSys/ASPLOS frame MoE as a serving tax (communication, expert offloading), ICML treats routing itself as the object of study** — training-free routers from expert eigenvectors ([Eigenvectors of Experts](https://openreview.net/forum?id=H1mxs9qGQX)), optimal-transport MoEfication ([DOT-MoE](https://openreview.net/forum?id=WrXyXq11Yc)), batch-aware routing without retraining ([Opportunistic Expert Activation](https://openreview.net/forum?id=bSLIDztFwx)), and dense→MoE conversion. The systems-adjacent work is still here — on-device MoE via lossless compression ([ZipMoE](https://openreview.net/forum?id=QUL7jp4QDQ)), expert merging + bit-packing ([PuzzleMoE](https://openreview.net/forum?id=D7pFsC7UAr)), speculative expert prefetch ([SpecMD](https://openreview.net/forum?id=hjmbstLYc9)) — plus the efficiency-accuracy scaling question ([Revisiting Efficiency–Accuracy Scaling](https://openreview.net/forum?id=iQRy0io4sb)). Judgment: the durable contribution here is better *routing*, which is upstream of every serving optimization the systems venues build — fix routing imbalance at the algorithm level and half the MoE "serving tax" ASPLOS/MLSys pay disappears.

## 7. The smaller algorithmic clusters: long-context memory, pruning, early-exit (43 papers)

**These are where ICML proposes architectural alternatives to the KV cache and the dense forward pass — bets that, if they land, make whole systems-venue subfields moot.** Long-context (14) is dominated by *recurrent/memory architectures* that sidestep the growing KV cache entirely ([Artificial Hippocampus Networks](https://openreview.net/forum?id=FlJnOeuN8C), test-time-gradient memory writes in [GradMem](https://openreview.net/forum?id=hoLdhnkP0P), infinite-horizon reasoning in [InftyThink+](https://openreview.net/forum?id=tyul8kXaJU)) — a fundamentally different answer than "compress the KV cache." Pruning/distillation (18) is standard structured pruning + low-rank ([A3 analytical low-rank attention](https://openreview.net/forum?id=aeeo8ZAftQ), [deterministic differentiable structured pruning](https://openreview.net/forum?id=64Kj8z626A)) with a reasoning-distillation lean ([Select to Think](https://openreview.net/forum?id=9AIG4VatqI)). Early-exit / dynamic depth (11) includes theory for when layers are skippable ([Skip-It?](https://openreview.net/forum?id=DqcPyN2gjD)) and reasoning-aware exit ([SpecExit](https://openreview.net/forum?id=5RMsiXvUqS)). Judgment: the memory-architecture line is the sleeper — if recurrent/hippocampal memory matches attention quality at long context, the entire KV-cache-compression industry (a third of both this report and MLSys) becomes a transitional technology.

## 8. Maturity: practicality vs research

**This is where ICML inverts the systems venues, and it's the most important caveat for acting on any of the above.** MLSys/ASPLOS skew toward deployed or deployable systems; ICML skews hard to research prototypes and — critically — a large fraction **requires training or changes the architecture**, so it is not drop-in at all:

- **Not deployable without training** (the largest group): every linear-attention architecture, most diffusion-LM work, QAT methods, MoE-routing changes, memory architectures. Their inference-efficiency claims are real but presuppose you can train or convert the model — a different cost basis than "turn it on in vLLM."
- **Training-free, drop-in** (the deployable slice): most KV-eviction and PTQ methods, training-free sparse attention, training-free routing ([Eigenvectors of Experts](https://openreview.net/forum?id=H1mxs9qGQX), [Opportunistic Expert Activation](https://openreview.net/forum?id=bSLIDztFwx)). These are the papers a serving team can use directly, and they cluster in KV + PTQ — exactly the overlap with the systems venues.
- **Reality-checks / benchmarks** (a healthy sign): [SPEED-Bench](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Rl2uQlCoQX) for speculative decoding, the [INT-vs-FP study](https://openreview.net/forum?id=1GIYHWO9S5), the [QAT scaling law](https://openreview.net/forum?id=fXr3uPr1G5) — ICML audits its own claims, like the systems venues do.

Takeaway: the *ideas* with the highest ceiling are here (linear attention, diffusion LMs, principled compression), but the *directly-usable* subset is the training-free KV/PTQ slice; everything else is a bet on a future training run.

## Method & distribution

**Selection.** ICML 2026 had ~6,341 accepted papers on OpenReview. A recall-oriented keyword pre-filter (inference/serving/KV/attention/quantization/MoE/decoding/long-context terms) cut this to 762 candidates; an LLM classifier (Claude Sonnet, relaxed inference-optimization scope) judged 366 in-scope. This two-stage funnel is the non-OpenReview-scale adaptation of the venue-trend pipeline — necessary because classifying all 6,341 with an LLM is disproportionate.

**Distribution of the 366 (classifier subfields, largest bucket wins ties):**

| Cluster | # | Cluster | # |
|---|---|---|---|
| speculative / parallel decoding | 59 | serving systems | 34 |
| KV cache | 49 | MoE inference | 27 |
| quantization | 48 | pruning / distillation | 18 |
| efficient attention | 46 | long-context / memory | 14 |
| (misc "other") | 59 | early-exit / dynamic depth | 11 |
| test-time-scaling | 1 | | |

**Biases, stated plainly.** (1) The keyword pre-filter can miss a paper whose inference angle isn't in its title/abstract keywords — recall is imperfect, so 366 is a lower bound. (2) Scope was **relaxed** to include algorithmic/architectural inference-efficiency work (linear attention, diffusion LMs, MoE routing), so a chunk of the 366 is not deployable without training — see §8. (3) ICML is an algorithms venue, so this is "what ICML accepted," heavily upstream of deployment; cross-venue comparisons to MLSys 2026 / ASPLOS 2026 are grounded in all three reports but reflect venue selection, not a field-wide count. (4) I read the four largest clusters (202 papers) in full and characterized the remaining 164 from titles + a sampled read — counts are exact, but per-paper claims outside the top four clusters are lower-confidence.
